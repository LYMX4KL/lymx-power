Cold-outreach scripts

Use as a starting point — make them your own
1

Cold-call opener (under 30 seconds)

"Hi [name], this is [your name]. I work with a small group of local Businesses on a shared loyalty network called LYMX. The reason I'm calling is short — Brew & Bean Café joined a couple months ago and they've added about 140 new customers a month from other Businesses on the network. I think it'd work really well for [Business name] too. Could I take 5 minutes to walk you through it?"
Why it works: leads with a peer (Brew & Bean), gives a specific number, asks for a small commitment (5 min). Don't drop "MLM" or "commission" in the opener — focus on Business outcome.
2

Email subject lines (high open rate)

• "Brew & Bean's loyalty story — thought of you"
• "Quick question about your loyalty program at [Business]"
• "Small Business idea from a neighbor"
• "(Re: 5 min about LYMX) — [Your name]"
Avoid all-caps, "FREE", or anything spammy. Best open rate: questions + named-peer references.
3

In-person walk-in (under 60 seconds)

"Hi — quick visit, not a sale. I'm a LYMX Partner — that's a shared loyalty network a few Businesses around here are part of. Brew & Bean signed up in February and saw [stat]. I left this one-pager — if it sounds interesting, my number's on it and I'd love to swing by when you have 10 min. Thanks for your time."
Always lead with "not a sale" if you're walking in. Drop the one-pager, don't push. Follow up in 3–4 days.
4

The follow-up email (after first meeting)

"[Name] — thanks for the time today. As promised, attached are: (1) the one-pager I mentioned, (2) the ROI math doc tailored for [their category], and (3) Brew & Bean's case study. Quick note: the Launch 25 program in [city] currently has [X] spots left. No pressure — just wanted you to have the info. Talk soon, [your name]."
Always include attachments. Always reference what they said in the meeting. End with a soft scarcity nudge if Launch 25 is open.

Objection handlers

12 most common · click any to expand
"I already have a loyalty program — Square Loyalty / Toast / Yelp."

The frame: they're not wrong — they DO have something. Don't argue. Acknowledge, then reframe.

Try: "Yeah, Square Loyalty is solid for tracking visits. The one thing it can't do is bring you customers from other Businesses — which is the part LYMX adds. Most LYMX Businesses keep Square as their POS and just plug LYMX in alongside. Doesn't have to be either/or."
"$850 is too expensive."

The frame: $850 sounds big. Break it into the parts so they see what they're getting.

Try: "Totally fair. Let me unbundle it — the $850 includes 3 months free subscription ($600 value), homepage feature spot at launch (worth several hundred in equivalent ad spend), email blast to all local LYMX customers, and the actual sign-up. Effectively the cash cost is closer to $250 once you net out the included value. Plus most peers break even within 6–8 weeks."
"I don't have time to set up another thing."

The frame: they're right that time is the bigger ask. Compress your ask.

Try: "Setup is genuinely 30 minutes. You give us your menu, we configure your dashboard, your existing POS keeps working. The most-time-intensive part — staff training — is a 5-minute video for cashiers. We can also send someone to do it on-site on launch day."
"What if it doesn't work?"

The frame: de-risk it.

Try: "Fair concern. Two things — first, our refund policy is 30 days no-questions. Second, we measure by Business outcome, not platform usage — if you don't see an ROI by month 3, we work with you on it (free promotional credits, optimization help). We have a refund policy posted publicly: getlymx.com/refund."
"This sounds like an MLM."

The frame: directly address — don't dodge.

Try: "I understand why it might look that way. Two big differences. One — Businesses pay $850 + $199/mo. They get a real product (POS, customer base, analytics). They're not buying inventory or recruiting other Businesses. Two — Partners (like me) earn commission for sign-ups, but we don't pay to be Partners. The Partner tier is a referral program, not a buy-in."
"I need to think about it."

The frame: always understand what they want to think about. Don't push, but probe.

Try: "Of course — what specifically do you want to think through? If it's the cost, I can walk you through the math. If it's whether your category fits, I can pull peer numbers. If it's just timing — when's a better moment to follow up?"
"Where else are you in?"

The frame: be honest about footprint. Don't oversell.

Try: "Right now we're concentrated in Las Vegas — 218 Businesses live, ~15,000 customers active. Phoenix and Reno are next, Q4 2026. The honest answer is we're new, but we're growing 38% month-over-month and the network density in Vegas is starting to compound."
