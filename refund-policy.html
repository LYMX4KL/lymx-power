📦 Returns & Refunds

Returns & Refund Policy

Effective April 30, 2026 · Last updated April 30, 2026 · Plain English version. Read alongside our Terms of Service.

The 60-second version

  • The Business sets the refund policy for the goods or services they sold you. LYMX is the loyalty layer, not the merchant.
  • If you return a purchase, the LYMX you earned on it can be reversed. The Business does this through their POS — you'll see a reversal in your wallet.
  • If you paid with LYMX and get a refund, the LYMX comes back to your wallet. Not as cash. LYMX is not redeemable for cash.
  • If you spot a wallet error (wrong amount, didn't recognize a charge, missing earned LYMX), file a dispute directly. We investigate within 1 business day.
  • LYMX itself never expires and we don't take it back arbitrarily. The only times your balance changes are: you earn, you spend, a Business reverses an issuance after a return, or a confirmed dispute is resolved.

1. Overview

LYMX is a loyalty network layer — we connect customers and Businesses through a shared promotional credit (LYMX). LYMX is not the merchant in your transaction. The Business you bought from sold you the goods or service. Their refund policy controls the underlying purchase.

What we control is the LYMX accounting on top: when you earn LYMX, when LYMX gets reversed because of a return, and when LYMX gets restored because of a refund.

This policy explains exactly how each scenario works, and what to do if your wallet doesn't reflect what should have happened.

2. Scenarios — what happens when

The most common situations, with what to expect from your wallet.

A

You returned a purchase you paid for in cash. You earned LYMX on it.

Most common scenario

The Business processes your return per their refund policy and gives you your cash back. They also reverse the LYMX they issued you for that transaction.

Your wallet: the previously-earned LYMX is deducted. Your overall balance returns to where it was before that transaction.

LYMX reversed
B

You returned a purchase. You paid with LYMX (fully or partially).

LYMX-funded purchase reversal

The Business processes your return per their refund policy. The portion you paid in LYMX is restored to your wallet — typically within 1-2 business days, sometimes instantly. Any cash portion is refunded to your original payment method (cash / card) by the Business.

What you don't get: we don't pay you out the LYMX as cash. LYMX comes back to your wallet, where you can spend it again at any LYMX Business.

LYMX restored to wallet
C

You bought something you can't return (e.g., a service that's been delivered).

Non-returnable

If the Business's refund policy says the item or service is non-returnable, no refund applies — and the LYMX you earned stays with you. We don't reverse LYMX on services that have been delivered just because you regret the purchase.

If you have an issue with the quality of the service, that's between you and the Business. We don't act as an arbiter for service-quality disputes.

LYMX stays
D

You think the Business should have given you more LYMX (e.g., promo wasn't applied).

Missing earned LYMX

If a promo multiplier was running but didn't apply, or you should have hit a sign-up bonus that didn't show up, file a customer dispute. We investigate within 1 business day. If we made an error, we credit the missing LYMX directly to your wallet.

3. How to request a refund / LYMX adjustment

Step-by-step, depending on what kind of refund you're asking for.

3.1 — For a refund of the underlying purchase (goods or service)

1

Go to the Business directly

The Business is the merchant. Their refund policy applies. Show them your receipt or transaction in your wallet.

2

The Business processes the return

They'll give you your cash back per their policy. They'll also issue a LYMX reversal in their POS — that takes about 30 seconds for them.

3

Check your wallet

The LYMX adjustment shows up in your activity history within 1-2 business days, often instantly.

3.2 — For a wallet error (didn't earn what you should have, or charged for something you didn't do)

1

File a customer dispute

Open the transaction in your wallet → tap Report an issue. Or go directly to customer-dispute.

2

We investigate within 1 business day

We email you with a case ID and an ETA. If it's clearly our error, we credit immediately.

3

If you believe it's fraud

Tap Lock my wallet now at the start of the dispute flow. No new transactions go through until our team unlocks. Your existing balance is preserved.

SLA: we respond to wallet disputes within 1 business day. Most resolve within 2 business days. Complicated cases (potential fraud, contested with a Business) can take up to 7 business days.

4. Bulk-gift orders (corporate gifting)

If your company purchased LYMX in bulk as gifts, the rules are slightly different:

  • Before delivery (gifts not yet sent to recipients): full refund available. Email bulk@getlymx.com within 7 days of order.
  • After delivery — unredeemed gifts: the LYMX is in recipients' wallets. We don't reverse-pull from their wallets. From a corporate accounting perspective, the gift was given.
  • Recipient never claimed: if a recipient doesn't claim the gift within 90 days, you can request the un-claimed value back as account credit toward your next bulk order. Not refundable as cash.
  • Volume-discount adjustment: if you cancel part of a bulk order before delivery and that drops you out of a tier, the remaining cards re-quote at the new tier.

5. Partner commissions & clawbacks

LYMX Partners earn cash and override commissions per the Partner Agreement. There are two scenarios where commission is reversed (clawed back):

  1. Sign-up bonus clawback: if a Business cancels their account in the first 30 days, the $500 sign-up bonus is reversed from the Direct Partner. Bonuses are paid 30 days after activation specifically to manage this.
  2. Recurring override clawback: overrides are paid monthly on actual subscription revenue. If a Business cancels mid-month, the proportional override for unbilled days is reversed.

Clawback events are itemized on your monthly Partner payout statement so you can see what reversed and why.

5b. Business cancellation: the 90-day wind-down

When a Business chooses to leave the LYMX network (different from going out of Business — they may simply want to stop offering LYMX rewards), here's what happens:

  1. Cancellation submitted through the dashboard. Effective immediately for new issuance — the Business stops issuing LYMX the moment cancellation is submitted.
  2. Outstanding balance auto-capped: the Business cannot issue new LYMX during the wind-down. They may still accept (redeem) LYMX from customers.
  3. Public listing flag: getlymx.com listing flips to a "Leaving the LYMX network — still accepting LYMX" banner with days-remaining countdown. The "Earn LYMX here" badge is removed.
  4. Customer-facing balance: customers see the remaining capacity at the departing Business (% of outstanding balance still redeemable). Transparent + helps customers spend down their balance.
  5. 90-day acceptance window: the Business continues accepting LYMX for 90 days OR until balance reaches $0 — whichever comes first.
  6. After 90 days: listing is removed from the public directory. Business can continue accepting LYMX privately (we don't strand customers) until balance reaches $0, but they're no longer searchable.
  7. Refund of unused subscription: any prepaid subscription beyond cancellation date is pro-rated and refunded.
  8. $850 sign-up: not refundable after the first 30 days (see section 4).

Selling your Business? Different from cancellation. The new owner takes over the LYMX account as part of the asset transfer — same balance, same customer relationships, same listing — provided they stay in the LYMX network. Email hello@getlymx.com at least 30 days before the sale closes so we can document the change of ownership.

6. What we never refund

To be straightforward about it:

  • Cash for LYMX. We don't redeem LYMX for cash, ever. LYMX is a promotional credit, spendable at LYMX Businesses, not money. (See disclaimer.)
  • Quality of goods or service. Disputes about whether your meal was good, whether your haircut was right, or whether a service was delivered as advertised — those are between you and the Business.
  • "Buyer's remorse" on a service that was delivered. If the service happened, the LYMX you earned on it is yours.
  • Bulk-gift cards already claimed by recipients. Once it's in their wallet, it's theirs.
  • Tax on a transaction. Sales tax is collected and remitted by the Business. We don't refund sales tax.

7. Disputes & chargebacks (cards)

If you used a credit card on a Business, payment-related disputes go through your card issuer's chargeback process — not LYMX. We're a loyalty layer; we don't process cards.

That said: if a chargeback is upheld in your favor, the Business will typically reverse the LYMX issued on that transaction (case A above). If they don't, file a customer dispute with us.

8. Changes to this policy

We update this policy when our practices change. Material changes are announced 30 days in advance via email to all customers and a banner on getlymx.com. Minor wording fixes are noted in the changelog.

9. Contact

Email support@getlymx.com for general questions about this policy. For specific wallet issues, the dispute flow is faster — it threads directly into our support queue with all the right context attached.

Plain promise

If something feels wrong, file a dispute. We'd rather investigate every weird-looking transaction than have a customer who feels unheard.

If you spot fraud, hit the lock-wallet button immediately. Your balance is safe; we'll sort it out from there.
