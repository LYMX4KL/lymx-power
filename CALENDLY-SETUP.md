# Calendly Setup — Founding 25 Discovery Call
**Goal:** Convert curiosity into a 30-min meeting where you can show the napkin math + dashboard + close to a Partner signup.

This file is the spec. Set up the Calendly event exactly as specified, paste in the question copy, and you're ready to share the link in every channel (LinkedIn DMs, cold emails, friends & family texts, IG bio).

---

## Event setup (Calendly dashboard)

### 1. Event Type

- **Type:** One-on-One
- **Name:** *Founding 25 — Vegas Partner Discovery*
- **URL slug:** `founding-25` (your link will be `calendly.com/{your-handle}/founding-25`)
- **Description (visible to invitee on the booking page):**

> 30-minute call to walk through what becoming a LYMX Founding Partner looks like. We'll cover:
>
> - The math (what you'd earn per business you bring on)
> - The dashboard (what Partners actually see and do)
> - The qualifying terms (5 direct activations to lock founding status)
>
> No pitch deck. Just the numbers and your questions.

### 2. Duration & buffer

- **Length:** 30 minutes
- **Buffer before:** 5 minutes
- **Buffer after:** 10 minutes (gives you time to enter notes immediately, which you will, every time)
- **Minimum scheduling notice:** 4 hours
- **Maximum days out:** 14 days (forces urgency — they book this week or next, not three weeks from now)

### 3. Availability

- **Timezone:** Pacific Time (Las Vegas)
- **Days:** Monday–Saturday
- **Hours:**
  - Monday-Friday: 9:00 am – 12:00 pm and 2:00 pm – 5:00 pm (skip lunch hour)
  - Saturday: 10:00 am – 1:00 pm only (light Saturday for sanity)
- **Sunday:** Block entirely. No exceptions during this campaign.

### 4. Location options

Let the invitee pick. Set up two locations on the event:

- **Phone call** — *Default to phone for cold prospects*. You call them at the number they enter. Less friction than Zoom; works on a walk.
- **Zoom** — *For warm leads who specifically asked.* Auto-generate a Zoom link via Calendly's integration. Useful if you want to share screen and demo the dashboard.

(**Don't** offer "in-person" through Calendly. Those happen separately as Founder Dinner invites or coffee meetings you set up directly.)

> Founder, LYMX Power

### 6. Reminder emails

Calendly sends reminder emails. Set them up:

- **First reminder:** 24 hours before the meeting. Use the default text — it's fine.
- **Second reminder:** 1 hour before. **Customize the body:**

> Quick reminder — we're meeting in an hour.
>
> {{Location Details}}.
>
> If anything's come up and you need to bump it, just reply to this email.
>
> — K

### 7. Cancellation / no-show policy

- **Allow rescheduling:** Yes (up to 4 hours before)
- **Allow cancellation:** Yes
- **Cancellation page text:**

> Sorry to miss you. If you want to grab a different time, here's my calendar: {{calendar link}}. If now's not the right time, no hard feelings — feel free to circle back when it makes sense.

## Booking-page questions (the intake)

These are the questions Calendly asks **before** the prospect lands a meeting. Keep it short — 4 questions max — or you'll lose people on the form.

### Question 1 (Required)
- **Type:** Short text
- **Label:** Your full name
- **Placeholder:** Maya Chen

### Question 2 (Required)
- **Type:** Short text
- **Label:** Best phone number
- **Placeholder:** +1 702 555 1234
- *(Used for both the phone-call option and SMS reminders.)*

### Question 3 (Required)
- **Type:** Multiple choice (single)
- **Label:** Which best describes you?
- **Options:**
  - Real estate / mortgage / insurance professional
  - Network marketer / direct-sales rep
  - Independent business consultant
  - Small-business owner (and curious about Partner side)
  - Vegas connector / community builder (chamber, BNI, etc.)
  - Other — I'll explain on the call

### Question 4 (Required)
- **Type:** Multiple choice (single)
- **Label:** How many Vegas small-business owners do you already know personally?
- **Options:**
  - Zero — I'm new to the small-business world
  - 1–5
  - 6–20
  - 21–50
  - 50+ — I have a real network

### Question 5 (Optional)
- **Type:** Long text
- **Label:** Anything you'd like me to know before we talk?
- **Placeholder:** Optional — concerns, questions, what you're hoping to get out of the call.

---

## Why these questions, not others

A few pitfalls to avoid:

- **Don't ask "have you done sales before?" as a yes/no.** It pushes "no" answers to feel disqualified and abandon the form. The implicit answer comes through Question 3 + 4 anyway.
- **Don't ask "what's your income goal?"** Way too early in the relationship. Save it for the call.
- **Don't ask for their LinkedIn URL.** Most people on phones can't easily paste a URL. You can find them yourself via name + phone before the call.
- **Don't add an "I have read the terms" checkbox.** Friction. They haven't agreed to anything yet — they're just booking a call.

The four questions above tell you, in 30 seconds before the call:

1. Who they are professionally (sets your tone)
2. What their network looks like (sets your expectations on activation speed)
3. Where the conversation should start

## Pre-call ritual (5 min before every call)

Calendly buffer gives you 5 minutes before the meeting. Use them. Open your tracking spreadsheet and review:

- Their answers to the four intake questions
- Their LinkedIn (search by name + Las Vegas)
- Any prior thread between you (cold email reply, DM history, mutual connection)
- Have ready: your napkin-math graphic, the Founding-25 PDF, the dashboard tab open in Chrome

Then breathe for 30 seconds. Then dial / launch Zoom on time. **Never call early; never call late.**

---

## Post-call ritual (10 min after every call)

Calendly's 10-min after-buffer is for this. **Do not skip it.**

In your tracking spreadsheet, log:

| Field | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Call date / time | Auto-filled from calendar |
| Their bucket | Hot / Warm / Cold / No |
| Network size estimate | From Q4 + your read |
| What they asked about | The 1-2 things they pressed on |
| Their objection (if any) | "Sounds like MLM" / "Not enough time" / "Math doesn't excite me" |
| Decided to sign up? | Yes (today) / Maybe (follow up by date) / No |
| Next action + date | "Send dashboard demo video by Wed" / "Coffee Tue 9am" / "Drop" |

If "Yes today," send them the Partner sign-up link **before they leave the call** (or within 5 min of hanging up):

> Just sent your Partner sign-up link via {{their channel — text/email}}. Takes 3 minutes. Once you're in, I'll text you our slack-style chat where the first Partners are talking shop. Welcome to the team.

If "Maybe — follow up," set a calendar reminder for the agreed date and **honor it**. Most "maybes" become "yes" with one well-timed follow-up.

If "No," send the friends-and-family deflection (FRIENDS-FAMILY-OUTREACH.md §5) and move on. **Don't** chase. Their network might still help — ask for one referral as you wrap up the call, then leave it.

## A note on Calendly free vs paid

The free tier of Calendly supports one event type and basic question sets. **That's enough for week 1.** If by week 2 you're booking 15+ calls a week, upgrade to **Calendly Standard** ($12/mo) for:

- Custom branding (your logo on the booking page)
- SMS reminders (instead of just email)
- Stripe / payment collection (not relevant here, but useful later)

Don't upgrade pre-emptively. Wait until volume justifies it.
