30-second elevator pitch

Memorize this. Deliver in 30 seconds.

"You know how every business runs its own loyalty program — points that expire, only work in one place, and customers never redeem? LYMX flips that. Customers earn one credit at any LYMX Business, spend at any other. When you reward a customer, you purchase the LYMX units from us at 80% — that 20% gap is your built-in customer-acquisition cost. When a customer brought in by the network spends LYMX at your store, we buy those units back from you at the same 80%. No ad spend. New customers walk in already holding LYMX. We're starting with 25 founding Businesses in Las Vegas — want to be one?"

★ Practice this until it flows. Every word earns its place.

★ The closer math

When the prospect asks "is $850 worth it?", you have one piece of math that answers everything. Memorize it.

🚀 The 1,500-customers-per-month math
Walk them through this: Their Business does ~$100K/month in volume. Average ticket $20. That's 5,000 customers/month. About 70% are repeats — meaning 1,500 brand-new customers walk through their door every month. Every one of them gets LYMX issued at checkout — meaning every one of them joins the LYMX network.

Then say: "Now imagine 10 Businesses signed up before you. That's already 15,000 LYMX customers in your metro — every one with redeemable LYMX in their pocket looking for a place to spend. Your $850 sign-up gets you a top-of-homepage feature for 30 days AND a one-time email blast directly to all of them. Replicating that with Yelp Ads or Google Ads would cost thousands. And the LYMX customers receiving the email already hold pre-funded credit. You're not buying impressions — you're buying customers who are ready to walk in."

Close with: "$850 once. Three months of service included. After that, $199/month. The math pays back in your first week."

Opening hooks (real customer pain)

The strongest way to open a cold conversation: lead with a pain the prospect has personally lived. These three are universal — any Business owner has felt at least one.

✈️ The "airline miles" hook
Use when: Talking to anyone who travels.

"Have you ever earned a ton of airline miles and then realized the cash price was actually cheaper than the points price? That's how broken loyalty programs are right now. LYMX fixes that — every LYMX is worth exactly $0.01, and customers can spend it anywhere on the network. No bait-and-switch on the value."
🍔 The "fast-food points" hook
Use when: Talking to a restaurant or food Business.

"How many people walk into a fast-food chain with thousands of unused points? They earn them but never redeem because the catalog only has items they don't want. Same problem with most loyalty programs — the rewards aren't really rewards. With LYMX, customers can spend on anything the Business sells, no item restrictions."
🏨 The "Hotel devaluation" hook
Use when: Talking to anyone who's tracked a rewards balance over years.

"Major hotel chains silently raise the points needed for the same room every year. Your 'value' shrinks while your account looks bigger. LYMX is locked at $0.01 face value — no devaluation possible because we'd break our own buy/sell mechanic. Customers know what their balance is worth, today and forever."

The Business-side hook

Once they get the customer pain, pivot to their pain.

💰 The "$2,000 / mo on ads" hook
The pitch: "Independent Businesses pay $400 to Yelp, $800 to Google, $600 to Facebook — easily $2,000 a month — just to be visible. Most of that is impressions, not customers walking in. With LYMX you pay $199/month subscription plus a 20% discount only when a network customer actually walks in and redeems. Total effective cost: roughly $320/month at typical volume. Less than 1/6 the cost — and every dollar maps to a real paying customer."

Follow-up question: "What are you currently spending each month on customer acquisition?" — listen carefully to the number. Reflect it back: "So you're paying [their number] / month for [Yelp/Google] impressions. LYMX would cost you [X] for actual paying customers."

FAQ & objection handlers

The 8 questions every Business asks. Answer crisply, move on.

"Is this multi-level marketing / MLM?"
No. The Multi-Generation Compensation rewards Partners for recruiting Businesses (4 layers deep), but Businesses themselves are not in the MGC chain — they're customers, not recruiters. There's no requirement to recruit, no minimum volume, no inventory to buy. A Business pays for software and gets customers. That's it.
"Is LYMX a cryptocurrency or stored-value account?"
No. LYMX is a promotional credit unit used exclusively within the Enterprise ecosystem. Not cash, not crypto, not a stored monetary balance. It's specifically structured this way to avoid money-transmitter regulation. The disclaimer is on every page of getlymx.com.
"What if I issue more LYMX than I take in?"
It means you've successfully sent customers into the network with money to spend, and other Partners have welcomed them. Your monthly settlement covers the difference. The 20% baked-in network discount is your customer-acquisition cost for those purchases. Most Partners settle near net-zero on LYMX flow over time; high-traffic Businesses (cafés, retail) end up net positive.
"What POS systems do you integrate with?"
Square, Shopify, Toast, Clover, and Stripe at launch. Receipt-scan fallback for Businesses that don't want to integrate at the POS level. Setup is part of the 5-step onboarding.
"What does the $850 sign-up actually cover?"
$850 = first 3 months of Enterprise service ($600 value) + $250 Activation Kit (portal setup, public listing, 10,000 LYMX promo credit, branded QR + window decal, onboarding call, POS integration support). Months 4 onward are $199/mo. Optional: a basic website built for $250 (50% off $500) if requested in the first 3 months.
"How is this different from Yelp / Square Loyalty / Toast Rewards?"
Those are single-business loyalty tools — points only redeemable at the issuing business, marketing only sold to the issuer. LYMX is a shared-value network — the credit works across every Partner, and the cross-business redemption is the marketing. Compare unit economics in the spreadsheet calculator.
"What if I want to cancel?"
No contracts. Cancel anytime from your dashboard. Outstanding LYMX you've issued stays redeemable for 90 days; final settlement bills at the end of that window. We don't claw back the $500 sign-up bonus from the Partner unless fraud.
"Why now? Why not wait until LYMX is bigger?"
Launch 25 pricing closes when we hit 25 Businesses. After that, sign-up jumps and the +2% lifetime override boost on the Partner side won't be available. Growth happens in the first network city by the founders — so the first 25 capture the lion's share of cross-business traffic for the first year, before competition increases.
