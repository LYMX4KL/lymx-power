How to connect QuickBooks

One-time setup. ⏱️ Total: ~10 min

1

Open Integrations in your LYMX dashboard

From your Business dashboard, click Settings → Integrations. Find the QuickBooks card and click Connect.

~30 sec
2

Sign in to QuickBooks Online & authorize

QuickBooks OAuth opens. Sign in. Authorize LYMX to write journal entries (we don't read your books or anything sensitive). Approve.

~2 min
3

Choose your accounting method

Two choices: (a) "Simple" — one journal entry per month. (b) "Detailed" — daily redemption entries + monthly settlement. Most Businesses pick (a). You can change later.

~1 min
4

Map LYMX items to your chart of accounts

We auto-suggest mappings based on the table above. Review and override anything that doesn't match your accounting setup. If unsure, default suggestions work for 90% of Businesses.

~5 min
5

Run a backfill (optional)

If you have prior LYMX activity that wasn't in QB, we can backfill journal entries for the past 90 days. Useful for cleaning up your books. Backfill runs in the background — takes 2–3 minutes.

~2 min
6

Verify in QuickBooks

Open QuickBooks Online → Reports → Journal Entries. Filter for entries with "LYMX" in the memo. They should appear with the correct categorization. Done.

~1 min

Frequently asked questions

Does this work with QuickBooks Desktop?

No — only QuickBooks Online. QuickBooks Desktop's API is much more limited. If you're on Desktop, we recommend exporting CSV from LYMX and importing manually (or migrating to QBO, which we recommend regardless).

What about Xero, FreshBooks, or Wave?

Xero integration is in beta — request access from integrations@getlymx.com. FreshBooks and Wave are on the roadmap (Q3 2026). For now, all of them work via CSV export.

Can my accountant access this?

Yes. Your accountant accesses LYMX entries through QuickBooks like any other entries. They don't need a separate LYMX login. They can also export the LYMX-tagged entries via QuickBooks reports.

What if I want to change the account mapping later?

Easy — go to Integrations → QuickBooks → Mapping. Change any line. Future entries use the new mapping. Past entries are unchanged (you can edit them in QuickBooks if you want them remapped).

Does this affect my LYMX settlement timing?

No. Settlement still hits your bank on the 5th of each month. The QuickBooks integration just creates the journal entry. Two separate things.

What if I make manual edits to LYMX entries in QuickBooks?

Manual edits are fine — we never overwrite them. Future entries continue to flow in unchanged. If you delete an entry in QuickBooks, we won't recreate it (we assume you have a reason).
