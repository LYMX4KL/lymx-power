14 follow-up templates that convert

Field-tested email and text templates for every stage of the Partner sales cycle. Personalize the details — but use the structure as-is.

3 follow-up rules

  • Personalize the first sentence. "Hey John, was thinking about [specific thing they said]" beats "Hi! Just following up." every time.
  • Always have a single specific ask. Vague follow-ups die. "Have 15 min Tuesday at 2pm?" works.
  • Stop after 4 follow-ups if no response. Move on. Re-engage in 90 days. Don't pester.

1 Day-after discovery thank-you

EmailPost-discovery
Send: Day after a discovery conversation — within 24 hours.
Subject: Following up — LYMX Hey [first name], Thanks for the few minutes yesterday. You mentioned [specific pain point they shared] — that's exactly the gap LYMX is built to close for [their type of Business]. I'd love to come back when you have 30 minutes. I'll bring real numbers from 3 Businesses your size and walk through what LYMX would look like for [Business name] specifically. Are you free Tuesday or Thursday afternoon this week? — [Your name] LYMX Power Partner [phone]
Why this worksYou're not pitching. You're following up on something specific they said and offering a concrete next step. The "Tuesday or Thursday" gives them a binary easy-to-answer choice.

2 Day-after discovery — text version

TextPost-discovery
Send: If they prefer texts — based on their stated preference.
Hey [first name] — appreciated the chat yesterday. Thinking about what you said about [pain point]. I'd love to bring back specific numbers next week. Tues or Thurs afternoon? — [Your name], LYMX
Why this worksSame logic, briefer. Texts get faster responses than emails — but only if the prospect agreed to texts.

3 "Yes, send me an email" follow-up

EmailPost-discovery
Send: When they brushed you off with "send me an email".
Subject: The numbers for [Business name] [first name] — quick one. You asked for an email so you could review LYMX on your own time. Most LYMX Partners send a deck. I'm not going to do that, because you'd skim it. Instead — I ran our revenue calculator on [Business name] using public estimates. Based on a $50K monthly revenue assumption, year-1 net contribution from LYMX would be around $54-84K. Caveats: depends on adoption, your category, and how much you'd run promos. That's the whole pitch. If those numbers seem worth a deeper look, I'd love 30 minutes to show real numbers from comparable Businesses. Tuesday at 2pm work? If not, totally fair — appreciate the time. — [Your name]
Why this worksYou acknowledged they're going to skim. You delivered specific numbers in line one. You set up a meeting ask without being pushy. Most "send me an email" replies get a cold "got it thanks" or no reply. This breaks the pattern.

4 1-week recheck after no response

EmailPost-discovery
Send: 7 days after first follow-up with no reply.
Subject: re: LYMX for [Business name] [first name] — quick bump. I think my last note may have hit your spam. Two weeks ago we chatted about LYMX for [Business name]. You mentioned [pain point]. I was hoping to schedule a 30-minute deeper look this week. Want me to drop by in person instead? I'm in [their neighborhood] this Thursday between 10-2. — [Your name]
Why this worksYou acknowledged silence without guilt-tripping. You offered a different format (in-person) which is harder to ignore. Setting a specific window gives them an easy yes.

8 14-day post-meeting "decision time" nudge

EmailPost-meeting
Send: 2 weeks after meeting with no decision.
Subject: Decision time on LYMX? [first name] — It's been a couple weeks since we talked. I want to be respectful of your time, so I'll be direct: The Vegas LYMX network is growing about 20 new Businesses per month. The first-mover advantage in your specific neighborhood goes to whoever joins first. There's currently no other [their category — coffee shop / salon / etc.] within 1 mile of you on the network. Why this worksYou introduced a real urgency (territorial advantage) without being fake. You gave them an easy out (saying no is fine). Ironically, this often closes deals — they realize they've been procrastinating.

14 Dead-deal post-mortem (after a "no")

EmailRe-engage
Send: Right after a Business explicitly said no.
Subject: Thanks for the consideration [first name] — Got it — appreciate you giving LYMX a real look. Two quick things: 1. If you ever change your mind, I'm here. Drop me a line anytime. 2. If you have 60 seconds — what was the deciding factor for "no"? Helps me get better at this. Either way, no pressure. Wishing [Business name] continued success. — [Your name]
Why this worksYou respected the no. You opened a door for later. You asked for honest feedback that 30% of the time reveals something fixable. Don't burn bridges — Vegas is a small town.

Sequencing rules

  • Total cadence — 4 follow-ups max: Day-1 → Day-7 → Day-14 → Day-30. After that, archive and re-engage at Day-90.
  • Don't blast. Each follow-up should reference something specific from the prior conversation.
  • Mix channels. If email isn't getting reply, switch to text or in-person.
  • Track in CRM. Mark which template you used. Patterns will emerge.
  • Test variations. Add your own subject lines. Compare open rates monthly.
