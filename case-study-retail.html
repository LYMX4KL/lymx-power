How a 1,200 sq ft bookstore turned LYMX redemptions into 42% of all transactions.

The Quiet Library, an independent bookstore in Summerlin, joined LYMX as Founding 25 Business #5. Eight weeks later, redemption transactions outpaced first-time-cash transactions for the first time in the store's 9-year history.

Alma Reyes opened The Quiet Library in 2017, betting that there was room in Summerlin for a small, curated independent bookstore. For 9 years she'd been right — slow growth, loyal regulars, the occasional book signing. But the math of small-bookstore retail is unforgiving. Foot traffic in low-frequency categories (books are something you buy 3–6 times a year, not weekly) is the daily challenge. Every empty hour is rent burning.

The setup before LYMX

The acquisition problem

  • ~14 customers per day average (variable: weekends busier)
  • Average ticket: $32 (mostly single-book purchases)
  • Customer return frequency: 4× per year for regulars, 1× for new customers
  • Marketing: Instagram (free), monthly newsletter (Mailchimp $20/mo), one bookstore-meetup event per month

The destination problem

  • Walk-in traffic relied on Summerlin foot traffic patterns
  • No reason for non-customers to discover the store
  • Online sales (Bookshop.org affiliate) decent but margin-thin
  • Slow weekday afternoons were the chronic pain point — empty store from 11 AM to 4 PM
"I wasn't trying to grow into a chain. I was trying to fill the empty hours. Marketing didn't work — for books, you need TIME more than ATTENTION, and Instagram doesn't give people that." Alma Reyes, Owner

Why LYMX was different

Unlike cafés or restaurants where LYMX customers earn frequently and redeem moderately, retail is the inverse — earn rarely, but the redemption is meaningful (a book is a $20–40 transaction). This makes retail Businesses NATURAL DESTINATIONS for redemption. Customers come in WITH a balance and leave with a book.

"Once I understood that customers were going to walk in with $15 of LYMX from coffee shops and want to spend it, I realized this was a foot traffic solution disguised as a loyalty program. That changed everything." Alma Reyes, Owner

The 8-week rollout

Week 1 · Feb 14, 2026

Sign-up + setup

$850 sign-up + $200/mo. Used Square as POS, layered LYMX on top. Set the default 5 LYMX/$ rate but added a special category boost: 2× LYMX on staff-pick books (highlighted shelf at front of store).

Week 2

First redemption arrives

A customer walked in mid-Tuesday afternoon with $14 of LYMX earned at Brew & Bean. Spent it on a paperback + an unrelated bookmark. First time Alma had a sale that day before 3 PM.

Week 3–4

Tuesday redemptions become predictable

By week 3, Tuesday afternoons had 4–6 LYMX redemptions per session. By week 4, it was 7–10. New customers who'd never been to the store walked in with money to spend.

Week 5

The "stack-and-spend" pattern emerges

Alma noticed customers were strategically saving up LYMX for The Quiet Library — a specific book they wanted, costing 800–1,500 LYMX. They'd accumulate from coffee shops, then come spend it on the targeted book. Books became a reward.

Week 6

Hit break-even on monthly cost

Net incremental revenue from LYMX customers: $1,420/mo. Subtract LYMX wholesale + subscription ($240): net +$1,180. The destination effect was bigger than expected.

Week 8 · Apr 11, 2026

Hosted first "LYMX Reading Hour"

Saturday morning event — 3× LYMX on all purchases for 2 hours. 28 customers, half new. Alma sold 47 books in 2 hours (average week: 80 books in 6 days). Best Saturday she'd ever had.

What surprised her

Three things stood out.

1. Books became a reward, not a purchase. Customers reframed their book buying. Instead of treating it as discretionary spending, they treated it as "spending earned LYMX." Mental accounting magic — the books literally felt like free, even though the same dollars left their wallet (just earlier, at a coffee shop). This made the purchase decision easier and ticket sizes larger. Customers spending LYMX bought 18% more books per visit than cash customers.

2. The 2× boost on staff picks worked harder than expected. Customers love a deal AND a recommendation. The combo (2× rate + curated pick) was 3.4× more likely to drive a sale than either alone. Alma now rotates staff picks weekly, treating it like a curated promo channel.

3. The cross-Business referrals had a category pattern. Coffee → bookstore was the most common LYMX flow. Customers earning at cafés in the morning would come spend at The Quiet Library on the same day. The bookstore became the "where do I spend my LYMX?" answer for the local Brew & Bean / Oakline / Sage & Stone customer set. Geographic concentration of LYMX Businesses creates these patterns.

"Coffee shop to bookstore is a real customer journey. I'd guessed this for years but couldn't prove it. The LYMX data made it concrete — and now I plan my staffing around it." Alma Reyes, Owner

What's next for The Quiet Library

Alma is now planning a regular LYMX Reading Hour event — every other Saturday, 3× rates for 2 hours, paired with a featured author or staff pick. She's also become a Partner — earning override commission on the 2 small Businesses she's referred to date. Her downline is small but growing.
