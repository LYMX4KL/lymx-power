Your first conversation with a Business owner

Whether you're walking in cold, calling a referral, or following up on a web inquiry — these are the first 6 minutes. The goal isn't to close. The goal is to qualify and earn a 30-minute formal meeting.

Discovery, not pitch

You are NOT here to pitch LYMX in this conversation. You're here to find out (1) is this Business worth your time, (2) is LYMX worth their time. Talk less, listen more. If at minute 4 you've been talking more than them, you've messed up.

1

The opening — 30 seconds

First 30 seconds · cold walk-in or warm intro
You"Hey [first name] — I'm Sienna with LYMX. I work with [3 nearby Businesses they'd recognize]. Got 2 minutes? I'm not here to sell you anything today, I just want to learn about your Business and see if there's a fit."
Them"Sure, what is LYMX?"
You"LYMX is a shared loyalty network — customers earn rewards at any local Business and spend them at any other. Think of it like the local-Business version of credit-card points. We have 218 Businesses on the Vegas network and 8,400 active customers. But before I get into details — tell me a bit about [Business name]."
Why this worksYou opened with disarming ("not here to sell"), name-dropped neighbors (social proof), explained LYMX in 1 sentence, then handed control to them. They expected a pitch; they got a question. They lean forward.
2

The 4 discovery questions — 3 minutes

Stages 2-5 · ~3 minutes

These four questions, in this order. Don't skip. Don't add. Each builds on the previous.

Q1"How long have you been open?"
WhyTells you stage. New = hungry for traffic. 5+ years = harder sell, focus on retention.
Q2"What's bringing you new customers right now? Yelp, Instagram, walk-in, word of mouth?"
WhyTells you their existing marketing spend and what works. You'll position LYMX as additive to whatever they say.
Q3"What POS do you use? I'm just curious about your setup."
WhyHard qualifier. If they're on a POS we don't support, you adjust expectations. If they say Square/Toast/Clover/Lightspeed/Shopify — easy install.
Q4"What's the one thing about your Business you wish you could change right now?"
WhyReveals their pain point. Their answer becomes the hook for your pitch in the next meeting. Common answers: "more lunch traffic," "fewer no-shows," "younger customers."
3

The bridge — 60 seconds

Connect their pain to LYMX without pitching

Based on Q4's answer, drop a 60-second relevant story. Don't pitch LYMX features — just tell them about a similar Business' result.

If they said "lunch traffic""Right. So Brew & Bean — coffee shop in Henderson — they had the same issue. Tuesday lunches were dead. They joined LYMX, ran a 2x LYMX Tuesday lunch promo. Tuesday revenue is up 40% over 6 months. That's just one thing they did."
If they said "younger customers""Yeah, the under-35 cohort doesn't really respond to traditional ads. UNLV Pizza Co joined LYMX last spring — within 2 months their UNLV student traffic went from like 8% of their customers to 28%. The students all use LYMX, that's how they discovered the place."
If they said "no-shows" or "fewer regulars""That's the retention game. Levi's Sushi in Summerlin had a serious lapse problem — folks coming once and never returning. They joined LYMX, used the network's lapsed-customer messaging tool. Their repeat-visit rate went from 24% to 41%. Strictly through the network."
Why this worksYou connected their stated pain to a real result from a comparable Business. Specific numbers, named Business, plausible mechanism. They start visualizing their own outcome.

5

The ask — 30 seconds

Earn the meeting OR exit gracefully
If qualified — ask for the meeting"This sounds like we could be a fit. I'd love to come back when you have 30 minutes — bring real numbers from Businesses your size, walk through what LYMX would look like for [Business name] specifically. What's a good time this week?"
If maybe — schedule the recheck"Makes sense — let me follow up in 90 days when you've got more steady traffic. Mind if I drop you a note at that point? What's the best way to reach you — text or email?"
If no — exit gracefully"Got it — sounds like the timing's not right. Appreciate you giving me the few minutes. Best of luck with [thing they mentioned]."
Why this worksYou earned a real meeting (with a hard time, not "sometime"), set expectations (real numbers, 30 min), and made it about THEM ("for [Business name] specifically") not about LYMX. If the answer was no, you didn't burn bridges.
⚠️

Recovery moves when discovery goes sideways

When the conversation breaks the script
"I don't have time right now""No worries. Two minutes — what's the best time of day for you? I'll come back then."
"Just send me an email""Of course. Quick question first — what's the one thing my email would have to address for you to actually open it?" (Then send a personalized email, not the generic).
"We tried something like this, didn't work""What did you try?" (Listen all the way through.) "Right — that's a different model. LYMX is open-loop, here's the difference..."
"I need to ask my partner / my wife""Totally fair. When can the three of us sit down? I'll come back then."
"What's the price?""$199 a month plus $850 sign-up. But I'd hate to anchor on price before you see what's in the package — can we do the proper meeting first?"
"How is this different from [competitor]?""Great question — we should walk through it together. Got 30 min later this week?"

Each recovery turns the objection into a meeting opportunity. Don't get into long debates standing at the counter — that's a meeting conversation.

After the conversation — log it

  1. In partner-crm: log the Business name, owner name, qualifier (Go/Maybe/No), pain point, next-step date
  2. If meeting set — schedule prep block 24h before
  3. If maybe — calendar reminder for the recheck date
  4. If no — note the reason (POS unsupported, owner hostile, closing) so future Partners don't repeat the visit
