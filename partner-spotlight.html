Sienna's story

Before LYMX

Sienna spent eight years as an enterprise SaaS Account Executive in Silicon Valley. By 2025 she was hitting quota every quarter — and burning out every quarter too. "I had a great paycheck and a small apartment I never saw."

When her father got sick in Las Vegas in early 2025, she relocated home to help. The corporate job didn't allow remote, and she found herself between gigs. A friend mentioned LYMX was opening an early Partner program for Las Vegas.

Why LYMX worked for her

"I'd never done anything that looked like network marketing. The thing that flipped it for me was: there was a real product. Local Businesses got real customers. There was no kit to buy, no inventory to push. I just had to find good Businesses and explain the model."

"My SaaS playbook transferred almost cleanly. Outbound + warm intros + a clear ROI pitch. The difference: I owned every Business I closed, forever. No quota reset on January 1."

Sienna's three rules

01

Pitch the Business owner, not the deal

"Most reps lead with $850 and the Partner overrides. I lead with: 'How do you bring back the customer who tried you once and forgot?' If they don't have a good answer, we're already 80% there."

02

Recruit Partners who already sell something

"My best downline are former B2B salespeople, real-estate agents, and insurance brokers. They already know how to ask for a meeting. I'm just teaching them a better thing to sell."

03

The follow-up is the job

"Most Businesses don't say no. They say 'send me more info.' My calendar is 60% follow-up calls and 40% first calls. After 90 days of following up, deals close. Period."
