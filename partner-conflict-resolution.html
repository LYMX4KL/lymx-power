When two Partners cross paths

Las Vegas is small. As LYMX grows, two Partners will eventually be talking to the same Business. Here's exactly how we handle it — fair, transparent, fast.

The 4 conflict-resolution principles

1. First-mover wins. Whoever logged the Business in their CRM first has primary claim — UNLESS they let it go cold for 60 days.

2. The Business chooses. If two Partners are credibly competing, the Business owner picks who they want to work with. They're the customer.

3. We resolve in 5 business days. No conflict drags on. Submit the dispute, we decide, everyone moves on.

4. Bad-faith tactics lose. Partners who poach actively-engaged prospects, lie about prior contact, or harass others — face commission penalties and possible Partner status review.

The 6 most common conflicts

Case 1 · Two Partners both have the same Business in their CRM

Most common
"I logged Brew & Bean on March 12. Then I find out Sienna logged them March 8 but never followed up. Who has the lead?"

The rule

First-mover wins, with cold-lead ruleThe earlier CRM entry has primacy IF they've had at least one verified contact (email, call, in-person) with the Business in the last 60 days. Otherwise the lead is "cold" and free for the next Partner.

Resolution

OutcomeCheck both Partners' CRM for last contact date. Whoever has the most-recent verified contact wins. If both are within 60 days — earlier-logged wins. If both are stale (60+ days no contact) — second Partner takes over fresh.

Case 2 · A Business contacted me, but another Partner pitched them last month

Common
"I got a cold inbound from Maria's Cafe. They want to sign up. Then Sienna messages me — she pitched them 3 weeks ago and they said no."

The rule

If the Business comes to YOU, you have right of first responseInbound leads beat outbound CRM entries. The Business chose to reach out — that's the strongest signal of intent.

Resolution

OutcomeYou handle the inbound, you keep the activation if they sign up. Out of professional courtesy: drop a one-line note to the prior Partner ("FYI, Maria's reached out — taking it from here"). No commission split. Sienna can re-engage them if they revert to inactive later.

Case 3 · Multi-location Business with different decision-makers

Medium
"I signed Park Avenue Tailor's Henderson location. The owner is opening 2 more locations and another Partner is now talking to them about those."

The rule

The signing Partner gets ALL future locations under that ownershipIf you signed the original Business, you get commission on every additional location they open under the same ownership. Multi-location is one Business in MGC tree terms.

Resolution

OutcomeYou keep the entire ownership. The other Partner backs off. They can refer their own DIFFERENT Businesses, but not poach within an existing ownership tree.

Case 4 · Business changes ownership while one Partner has been engaging

Medium
"I've been talking to Old Town Pizza for 4 months. Today I find out they sold the business last week to a new owner — and another Partner already pitched the new owner."

The rule

Ownership change resets the leadThe new owner is a new decision-maker. Whichever Partner reaches them first AFTER the ownership change has primary claim. Prior conversations with the old owner don't transfer.

Resolution

OutcomeCheck who contacted the new owner first post-change. They have the lead. Recommend amicable conversation: "I had a long history with the old owner — would love to be considered, but defer to whoever they want to work with."

Case 5 · Friend / family / personal connection

Medium
"My cousin owns a salon. Another Partner is now also talking to her. I never logged her in CRM because I figured I'd just sign her up casually."

The rule

Personal connections need to be in CRM to be claimedThe "I knew them already" defense doesn't hold up unless logged. CRM entries are how we track lead ownership. If you didn't log it, you don't own it.

Resolution

OutcomeIf the cousin chooses you, fine. If she chooses the other Partner because they pitched first, also fine. The Business owner decides. Lesson: log every prospect, even friends and family. CRM is your protection.

Case 6 · Allegation of poaching or bad-faith behavior

Rare
"Another Partner has been telling Brew & Bean's owner that I'm 'unreliable' and 'won't follow through.' That's unfair and false."

The rule

No badmouthing other Partners. Period.Sales is competitive. Winning by tearing down a colleague is not. Allegations of badmouthing trigger formal review. Repeated violations = commission penalty + possible Partner status revocation.

Resolution

OutcomeSubmit conflict-resolution form with evidence (text screenshots, witness statements, email forwards). LYMX legal team investigates within 5 business days. If verified: offending Partner forfeits any commission on Brew & Bean if they later sign + receives written warning. Repeat offense: probation or removal.

How to file a conflict-resolution request

1

Try direct conversation first

Most "conflicts" are misunderstandings. Reach out to the other Partner directly: "Hey, I've been talking to X — what's your status with them?" 80% resolve here.

2

If unresolved, file the form

Dashboard → Help → Conflict resolution. Provide: Business name, your CRM entry date, last contact date, the other Partner involved, what you'd like the outcome to be.

3

LYMX team reviews — 5 business days

We pull both Partners' CRM data, check timestamps, may interview the Business owner. We're looking at facts, not feelings.

4

Decision delivered to both Partners

Written ruling with reasoning. Includes any commission allocation, lead reassignment, or warnings.

5

Appeal — once

If you disagree, you have 7 days to appeal in writing. Appeal goes to senior leadership. Decision after appeal is final.

🚩 Behaviors that hurt your case

  • Backdating CRM entries to falsely claim earlier contact
  • Telling the Business owner negative things about the other Partner
  • Pitching aggressively to a Business you know another Partner is engaging
  • Withholding context to manipulate the resolution
  • Personal harassment of another Partner
  • Threatening to leave the network if you don't get the lead
  • Asking the Business owner to "lie" about timing

FAQ

Does LYMX have territories assigned?

No formal territories — Vegas is small enough we don't need them. Your "territory" is wherever you've logged active leads in CRM. See territory-program for context.

What if I just want to refer the lead to another Partner?

You can voluntarily transfer leads in CRM. Settings → Transfer lead. Original logger keeps a 25% commission split for one year as a finder's fee.

Can a Partner own a Business in another Partner's downline?

Generation tracking is separate. If you're G2 to Sienna, and you sign Maria's Cafe, that's YOUR Direct Production — Sienna gets your override per MGC rules, but the Business is in your tree.

How are commissions split when there's genuine 50/50 ambiguity?

Rare, but does happen (e.g., truly simultaneous CRM entries). Default split: 60/40 in favor of whoever the Business prefers. If Business has no preference: 50/50 commission share for first year.

What if I think the resolution decision was wrong?

Appeal once, in writing, within 7 days. After appeal, decision is final. Continued contesting can affect your standing.

Do conflict resolutions become public?

No — never named publicly. Aggregated stats (e.g., "0.4% of Partner activity hits formal resolution") may be shared. Specifics confidential.
