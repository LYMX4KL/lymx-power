# Founding 25 Campaign Brief
**Code name:** "First 25 in Vegas"
**Window:** Mon May 11 → Sun June 7, 2026 (4 weeks)
**Budget:** $500–$3,000

## 1. Campaign Overview

A 4-week sprint to recruit and onboard the first wave of LYMX Power Partners in Las Vegas, leveraging Kenny's 16+ existing warm leads, personal network, and organic social presence. The "Founding 25" framing creates scarcity — a permanent grandfather perks tier reserved for the first 25 Partners who hit the qualifying threshold (5 Direct business activations).

**Primary objective.** **Sign up 50 LYMX Partners in Las Vegas by Sunday June 7, 2026** — enough volume that the race for the first 25 qualified Founding spots becomes self-sustaining beyond the campaign window.

**Secondary objectives.**

1. Activate 100+ businesses on LYMX (each Partner needs 5 Directs to qualify; 50 Partners × 2 average activations = 100+ businesses).
2. Get the first 5 Partners fully qualified as Founding 25 by June 7 — to use as case studies in week-4 messaging and the next campaign.
3. Build a Vegas-local content library (3 hero stories, 12+ short videos, 5 case-study posts) that keeps producing leads after the sprint ends.

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary: Vegas Side-Hustler / Sales-Native Connector

A Las Vegas resident, age 28–55, with sales DNA and an existing network of small-business owners. They make their living (or want to) by connecting people. Examples:

- Real-estate agents, mortgage brokers, insurance agents
- Network marketers / direct-sales reps (Mary Kay, Amway, Rodan + Fields, etc.) looking for a B2B alternative
- Independent business consultants, marketing freelancers, small-biz CPAs
- Active chamber members, BNI / Toastmasters / Rotary regulars
- Gym owners, salon owners, coaches with peer-business networks

**Pain points.** Their current side hustle is saturated, MLM-style, or commission-only with no recurring revenue. They've watched friends "miss the early window" on Uber, DoorDash, Bitcoin — and they want to be early on something local and tangible. They're tired of pitches that feel like Ponzi schemes.

**Motivations.** Cash-up-front per activation ($750 per business they bring on, paid for life), a meaningful local impact (helping Vegas small businesses survive), early-mover prestige (Founding 25 status), recurring revenue from the 11% commission that compounds over time, ownership (own their book of businesses).

**Where they spend time.** LinkedIn (mid-day scrolling), Instagram and Facebook local-business groups, weekly chamber/BNI breakfasts, coffee at independent cafes, gyms before 8am.

**Buying stage.** Mostly cold to LYMX. Will be aware → consideration → decision in a single 30-min coffee meeting once they see the math.

### Secondary: Existing Small-Business Owner with Spare Hustle

A Vegas business owner (cafe, salon, gym, consultant) who already loves serving local customers and has a few peer-owners they'd happily refer. They sign up as both a Business AND a Partner — they activate themselves first (1 Direct), then refer 4 peers. Lower volume than primary, but very high conversion: their warm intros close near-100%.

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **MLM perception** — prospects hear "recurring income from referrals" and think pyramid scheme. | High | Lead with "you earn it, you don't buy it." Show the business value (real software, real loyalty rewards driving real customer behavior) before the comp plan. Use the dashboard demo, not a sales script. |
| **Warm-lead underperformance** — fewer than 8 of 16 close. | Medium | Have cold-email and event channels already running by week 2 to compensate. Don't over-anchor on warm leads. |
| **Business activation lag** — Partners sign up but can't get businesses to activate, so no one qualifies for Founding 25 by week 4. | High | Build a Partner Playbook (already exists per memory) with cold-call scripts, email templates, talk tracks. Coach the first 5 Partners 1-on-1 in week 1. Their wins become week-3 case studies. |
| **Founder Dinner under-attendance** — fewer than 6 RSVPs. | Medium | Send invites by week 2 Monday. Personal text follow-up to each invitee 48 hours before. If under 6 confirmed by Tuesday week 3, downgrade to "Founder happy hour" at a partner business. |
| **Burnout** — 4-week sprint at this pace is brutal. | High | Block Saturdays as no-content / no-meetings (except the 1 BNI visit). Sundays for review only. |
| **"LYMX = currency" misframe in content** | Medium | Hard rule: review every piece of content before posting. LYMX is rewards / loyalty rewards / credits / points — never currency, money, or crypto. |
