The day, hour by hour

T-1 day · evening

Pre-launch sanity check

~20 min

The night before launch — light setup, no live transactions yet. Just confirming everything's wired correctly.

  • Log into your LYMX Business dashboard and confirm: menu items synced, multipliers set, payment method on file
  • Print the window cling + table tents — stick the window cling at eye level on your front door
  • Run a $1 test transaction (refund yourself afterward). Confirm LYMX issuance shows up on the dashboard within 30 sec
  • Send the cashier-training video to your team in a group text. Video link · 6 min
Why this matters: 90% of Day 1 issues happen because something basic was off the night before. Spend 20 min the day before — sleep better.
7:30 AM · before opening

Staff huddle

~10 min

Before the doors open, gather your team. Don't lecture — keep it concrete.

  • Show them the register card ("Tap to pay" prompt)
  • Walk through the new flow: customer says "I have LYMX" → you scan their QR → ring up Square as normal → LYMX auto-issues
  • If a customer doesn't have LYMX, hand them the table tent — sign-up is 30 sec at the table
  • Make it clear: there's no quota, no commission for cashiers, no pressure. Just answer "what is this?" if asked.
8:00 AM · doors open

First transactions

~30 min of attention

The first hour, watch from a distance. Don't hover — but be available if the cashier has a question. Most owners report this hour feels surprisingly normal.

  • Check your dashboard twice during this hour. Confirm transactions are coming through.
  • If something's off (transaction not appearing, customer can't redeem), call LYMX support directly — we have a Day 1 Slack channel for new Businesses
  • Take a screenshot of your first LYMX transaction. Send it to whoever helped you decide to sign up — your Partner, your friend, etc.
10:00 AM

Tell your customers

~20 min

Now that the system's working, announce it publicly. Don't make it big — soft launch.

  • Post the announcement social post from the Marketing kit (Instagram + Facebook)
  • If you have an email list, send the "We're now on LYMX" email template
  • Pin the announcement to your IG profile + add a story
  • For the first week, leave a stack of table tents at every table or by the register — works better than asking
Tone: understated is better than salesy. "We joined a shared loyalty network — earn here, spend anywhere on the network." That's all.
12:00 PM · lunch rush

Second-shift training

~5 min per cashier

If your morning crew is different from your lunch crew, the first lunch shift is your second training opportunity. Same flow as the morning huddle.

  • Pair them with the morning cashier for 10 min if possible — peer training is way faster than owner training
  • Watch the first 3–5 LYMX transactions of the lunch crew. Coach if the rhythm is off.
3:00 PM · afternoon check-in

First half-day review

~10 min

Halfway through Day 1. Open the dashboard and look at the actual numbers — not to evaluate, just to learn the rhythm.

  • How many LYMX transactions so far?
  • How many new wallet sign-ups?
  • Are there any redemptions yet (these come from network customers — exciting first sign of network value)?
  • Any cashier confusion or customer questions repeated more than once?
6:30 PM · close-of-day

End-of-day digest

~15 min

After close, do the EOD review. This is the most important habit you'll build — most successful Businesses do this every Friday for the first 4 weeks.

  • Check the daily digest email (auto-sent at close — delivery to [your email] by 7 PM)
  • Note any cashier issues + write a quick "tomorrow we should…" line
  • Reply to any customer questions on social
  • Optional: post a "first day" thank-you on social ("142 of you earned LYMX with us today — thanks for being early")
+1 week · Day 7 review

One-week debrief

~30 min

One week in, do a real review. By now you should have meaningful numbers and a sense of the cashier rhythm.

  • Pull your first analytics view — adoption rate, transaction count, redemption pattern
  • Set your first multiplier promo (typical first week 2 promo: 2× on a single bundle item)
  • Review your benchmarks for the first time — even though you only have 7 days of data
  • Schedule the first recurring 30-min weekly review on your calendar

What NOT to do on Day 1

  • Don't change your menu or prices. Run LYMX in parallel with everything else exactly as-is for the first week. Isolate the variable.
  • Don't run a multiplier promo on Day 1. You need a baseline before you start running promos. Wait until week 2.
  • Don't pressure cashiers to push sign-ups. Forcing it makes the experience feel scammy. Customers sign up at 35–55% naturally with just signage.
  • Don't ask your support team to memorize anything. If a customer has a question, the answer is "I'll help you sign up — it's easier to show you." That's the only script needed.
  • Don't expect a redemption on Day 1. Customers earn first, redeem later. Your first redemption usually comes on Day 4–7.
