## 5. The objection-handler cheat sheet

> "Sounds like an MLM."

LYMX is technically a multi-generation comp plan, but with two big differences from MLM red flags. (1) Compensation is paid by the platform on real Business activity — not on partner-to-partner sales. There's no kit to buy, no autoship, no inventory. (2) The 4-generation structure is bounded — overrides cap at G3 — and the bonuses are flat dollars per Business activation, not percentage of a downline's signup fees. The Federal Trade Commission's "primary money source = product sales to end users, not recruitment" test is met by design.

> "Why would I do this instead of selling Square / Toast / Clover terminals?"

Those POS vendors pay you once on the install and reset to zero. LYMX pays 11% recurring on the Business's monthly fee FOREVER while you stay active. Plus you stack overrides across your whole tree, not just your direct sales. And we're 1/100th the team size, so the territory is wide open.

> "What's the catch on the Founding 25?"

The Founding 25 perks are EARNED, not claimed. You have to activate 5 Businesses yourself before the lifetime perks lock in. We made it earned-not-bought specifically to prevent gaming. If you're not a real connector, you won't qualify, which is fine — that means the perks go to people who actually built the network.

> "How do customers actually use LYMX?"

Customer signs up free at getlymx.com. They walk into any LYMX Business, scan the QR at checkout (or the Business enters their phone). The transaction credits LYMX to their wallet at the published rate (e.g. 5 LYMX per $1). They can spend that LYMX at ANY other LYMX Business — even one in a different category. We charge the Business an issuance fee, settle weekly. From the customer's view: it's just a really cool loyalty card that works everywhere.

> "What if my prospects say no?"

They mostly will. Cold conversion to a $850 setup fee + monthly is single-digit. But our average partner who hits 5 activations does so in 60 days, and one Business activation pays out $750 + 11% × $199 × 12 = $1,012.92 in year 1 alone. You only need a handful to make it economically sane. Lean on warm intros, don't go door-to-door.

## 6. Quick math you can show prospects

A Partner who activates 10 Businesses (Direct, no downline yet):
  - Sign-up bonuses: 10 × $750 = $7,500 (one-time, Founding 25 rate)
  - Recurring: 10 × $199 × 11% × 12 months = $2,626.80 / yr
  - **Total year 1: $10,126.80** before any downline activity

A Partner with 5 directs and a small downline (5 G1, 3 G2):
  - Direct overrides (5 active): 5 × $199 × 11% × 12 = $1,313 / yr
  - G1 overrides (5): 5 × $199 × 3% × 12 = $358 / yr
  - G2 overrides (3): 3 × $199 × 2% × 12 = $143 / yr
  - **Just from existing tree, before adding any new directs: $1,814 / yr recurring**

Use these numbers. They're real and they're conservative.
