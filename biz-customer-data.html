What customer data you see — and don't

LYMX is built on a privacy-first principle: the network knows the customer, the Business knows the relationship. You see enough to run loyalty effectively. You don't see enough to harvest customer data. Here's the exact line.

✅ What you see

  • Wallet ID (anonymous)A unique customer identifier you can use across visits. Like "C-4827."
  • First name onlyFor greetings at the register. Not full name.
  • Visit history at YOUR BusinessEvery transaction they did with you. Date, amount, items if your POS sends them.
  • LYMX issued + redeemed at YOUR BusinessFull earn/spend ledger for your specific store.
  • Customer tags they opt into"Vegan", "gluten-free", "regular at my Business" — only what they self-tag and consent to share.
  • Aggregate cohort data"You have 312 active LYMX customers, average visit 1.4x/month."
  • Birthday (month/day, not year) — IF they shared itCustomer-set in their profile, only if they opted in.
  • First-visit flag"This is their first time" so you can extend a welcome.

❌ What you DON'T see

  • Phone numberYou scan QR; never see digits.
  • Email addressWe don't even ask customers for one at sign-up.
  • Last nameFirst name only at register.
  • Home addressNever collected.
  • Birth year / ageBirth month/day only, no year.
  • Race, gender, demographicsNever collected.
  • Visit history at OTHER BusinessesYou don't know they also visited the salon down the street.
  • How much LYMX they have totalOnly what they earned at YOUR Business.
  • Their full transaction historyJust yours.
  • Other Businesses they're loyal toTheir network behavior is private.
  • Where they live or workNever collected.
  • Payment card detailsCard numbers stay with your POS, never touch LYMX.

Why we built it this way

The traditional loyalty industry is selling customer data. We don't. The pitch we made to customers — "join LYMX, your data stays yours" — only holds if Businesses don't have access to PII.

  1. Customers trust the system more when they know individual Businesses don't see their full activity
  2. Higher network adoption means more LYMX customers in YOUR Business, even if you can't email them
  3. Privacy-by-design = compliance with future regulations (CCPA, GDPR, state-level laws)
  4. You still get marketing reach — through LYMX network email blasts, not direct customer email lists

Common scenarios

"I want to email my regular customers about a new menu"

You don't have customer email addresses. But you can use the network blast feature.

SolutionDashboard → Marketing → "Send to my LYMX customers." We send the email/text on your behalf to wallets that have purchased from you. Customer's PII never touches your hands.

"A regular customer hasn't visited in 6 weeks — can I reach out?"

Yes — through LYMX, not directly.

SolutionDashboard → Customers → Lapsed (auto-segmented) → Send "we miss you" message. Goes through LYMX as a wallet notification. They see it, you don't see their info.

"Can I export my customer list to my own CRM?"

Partial export only — nothing that identifies an individual.

Solutionbiz-data-export.html lets you export wallet IDs + behavior data. No phone, no email, no name. If you want re-identifiable data, you have to have customer's explicit consent (rare).

"A customer asked for a birthday discount — how do I know it's their birthday?"

If they shared their birthday in their wallet profile, your dashboard flags them.

SolutionDashboard → Customers → tap wallet ID. If birthday is shared, you'll see "Birthday: April 18" (no year). Most customers share month/day for the bonus.

"Police are investigating a customer — can you give them data?"

Only with a valid subpoena, and you're not in the loop.

SolutionDirect law enforcement to legal@lymx.com. We process subpoenas. You shouldn't be a middleman.

"What if a customer disputes a transaction — do I see their info to investigate?"

You see what's needed for the dispute, no more.

SolutionDispute reveals: wallet ID, first name, transaction date/amount, customer's claim. Doesn't reveal phone, email, address. Enough to investigate, not enough to retaliate.

FAQ

Can I add custom data fields to customer profiles?

You can add free-text notes (e.g., "always orders the special") that only your Business sees. The customer can request to view all notes you've made about them via their data export tool.

What happens to customer data if I leave the network?

Wallet IDs and historical data become read-only. You can still pull historical reports for 12 months after exit. After that, your dashboard is closed and data is archived.

Do customers know what I see about them?

Yes — every customer can request a full report of "what data Business X has on me." Transparency is non-negotiable.

Can I share customer data with my marketing agency?

Only what customers consent to share. Standard data export contains no PII so you can share that freely.

What if I want to call a customer about a service issue?

You can request "callback request" through dashboard. We notify the customer; they decide whether to respond. You don't get their number unless they choose to share.

Can I track which menu items a customer orders?

If your POS sends item-level data to LYMX, yes — at the wallet ID level. You'll see "wallet C-4827 orders the chicken sandwich 60% of the time" but not the customer's name attached to that pattern.
