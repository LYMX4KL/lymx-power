The LYMX cooperative charter

A loyalty rewards network only works if the people in it actually cooperate. This is the social contract — what we expect from each other, and what we expect from ourselves as the platform.

Preamble

The LYMX network exists because local Businesses, customers, and Partners are stronger together than alone.

Big-box retailers and chain stores have data, scale, advertising budgets, and platforms that independent local Businesses don't. LYMX is one answer to that imbalance — a shared rewards network where what you earn at one local Business is useful at every other local Business.

For that to work, the people inside the network have to act cooperatively, not extractively. This document is the charter that defines what cooperation looks like.

Article 1

Three roles, no others

The LYMX network has exactly three roles. We define them carefully and don't dilute them with new tiers or sub-categories.

Customer

Anyone with a LYMX wallet. Earns rewards on purchases, redeems anywhere on the network. Free to join.

Business

A local merchant who joins the network to issue + accept LYMX. Pays a subscription. Sets their own rate.

Partner

A representative who recruits Businesses to the network. Earns commissions via MGC.

That's it. We don't invite "VIP customers" or "premium businesses" or "executive partners." If we ever do introduce a tier, it's a designation WITHIN one of these three roles — not a new role.

Article 2

One LYMX, fixed redemption value

Every LYMX has the same redemption value: 1 LYMX = $0.01 in goods or services at any participating Business. The redemption value is fixed across the network and across time.

This isn't an investment vehicle. LYMX doesn't appreciate. It's not a currency. It's a promotional credit unit — useful only for redemption at participating Businesses. We chose this design so customers spend their rewards instead of hoarding them, which keeps the network active and valuable for everyone.

If a loyalty unit can appreciate, it stops being a reward and becomes an investment. People hoard investments — they don't redeem them.
Article 3

The Business owns the customer relationship

LYMX is the rails. Businesses are the destination. The platform never gets between a Business and their customer.

Businesses set their own LYMX rate (commonly 5/$1 or 3/$1). Businesses run their own promos. Businesses see anonymized activity at their location only. Businesses can leave the network anytime with a 90-day wind-down.

We don't sell customer data. We don't insert ourselves into Business-customer interactions. We're infrastructure, not a middleman.

Article 4

Customers own their data

Every customer can see, export, correct, or delete their data anytime. We collect the minimum needed to run a wallet. We don't sell to advertisers, data brokers, or even Businesses on our network.

See customer-privacy-rights.html for the full breakdown of what we have and how to exercise your rights.

Article 5

Partners build the network — and are rewarded for it

Partners aren't traditional sales reps. They're network builders. They recruit Businesses they personally vouch for, and earn commissions through MGC across 4 generations as those Businesses succeed.

The cooperative ethos: Partners help each other. New Partners learn from senior Partners. Founding 25 Partners (the first 25 to hit 5 Direct activations) commit to ongoing cooperative training to keep the next generation strong. See Founding 25 qualifying program.

Partner conflicts are resolved fairly using documented rules. We don't tolerate poaching or badmouthing.

Article 6

The platform is accountable

LYMX (the platform / company) commits to:

  • Transparency. Public stats (active customers, Businesses, LYMX issued/redeemed). Annual transparency report. Public roadmap.
  • Stability. Fixed redemption value. No surprise rate changes. 90-day wind-down protections.
  • Privacy. No data sales. Strong default protections.
  • Fairness. Same terms for similar Businesses + Partners. No backroom deals.
  • Independence. Privately owned, no plans to be acquired by a chain store or platform that would extract from local Businesses.
Article 7

Reciprocity

Every member of the network — customer, Business, Partner — gets value AND gives value. Customers get rewards but also become advocates for participating Businesses. Businesses get traffic but also issue LYMX that brings traffic to OTHER Businesses. Partners earn commissions but also bring real Business relationships into the network.

If anyone in the network is taking value without giving any, the network fails. Reciprocity is what keeps it alive.

Article 8

Local first

LYMX is for independent, owner-operated, locally-rooted Businesses. We're skeptical of national chains — not hostile, but slow to onboard them in a way that would dilute the local-network ethos. (See white-label for our enterprise approach, which is structured to protect the local network.)

The network's value is the network's locality. A Vegas-only network where customers can earn at the coffee shop and spend at the salon down the street is structurally different — and structurally better for local Businesses — than a national rewards program.

Article 9

Boring infrastructure, exciting outcomes

We don't want LYMX itself to be exciting. The platform should fade into the background — POS integration that just works, weekly settlement that just arrives, customer wallets that just function. Excitement happens at the Business level: a Tuesday lunch packed with new customers, a salon owner who finally has tools to compete with the chain.

If you ever feel like LYMX is "in the way" of you running your Business or being a customer, that's a bug, not a feature. Tell us.

Article 10

The cooperative grows or shrinks based on contribution

We protect ongoing contribution. Founding 25 Partners maintain their grandfather perks by continuing to help new Partners + Businesses succeed. Businesses with active engagement grow with the network. Customers who actively spend their LYMX get the deepest value.

The flip side: passive extraction (signing up and never engaging) gradually loses access to growth perks. We don't kick people out — but we reward those who reciprocate.
