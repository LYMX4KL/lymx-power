Time your redemption around bigger meals

3% LYMX network fee applies on redemption. So redeeming 1,000 LYMX ($10) takes a $0.30 cut, but redeeming 5,000 ($50) takes $1.50. The fee is fixed % so it doesn't matter mathematically — but psychologically you'll feel the value more on bigger redemptions.

How: Save up for a nice dinner or a salon visit instead of $1 off coffee.

Group dining? Be the LYMX-payer

If you split a $200 dinner 4 ways, only the person who pays the bill earns LYMX on the full $200. Volunteer to be that person: put the bill on your card, have your friends Venmo you their share back. You end up $50 out of pocket but you earned 1,000 LYMX (worth $10 of redemption at any LYMX business in the network). Your friends are square with you on the dinner — and the LYMX is your reward for being the bill-payer.

How: Put the full bill on your card. Each friend Venmos you their portion of the food cost. The LYMX lands in your wallet automatically — and stays there. (LYMX is non-transferable by design, so it always belongs to whoever pays the bill.)

FAQ

Is there a daily/monthly cap on how much LYMX I can earn?

No customer-side cap. Businesses cap their own LYMX issuance, but you'll never hit "you've earned too much" as a customer.

Does LYMX have a tier system or status level?

Not currently. Every customer earns the same rate at the same Business. We may add status perks later but nothing today.

Can I redeem LYMX for cash?

No — LYMX is redeemable for goods/services at network Businesses, not for cash. This is a regulatory thing (cash-equivalent loyalty programs become securities).

What's the most LYMX someone has earned in a year?

Top customer in 2025 earned 47,800 LYMX ($478) — heavy daily user across 14 Businesses. Median active customer earned ~$162.
