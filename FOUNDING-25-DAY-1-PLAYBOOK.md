## Step 1 — List your 30 prospects

Before you send a single message, write down 30 names. Vegas-area only. Small business owners only. People you can warmly contact (you've met them, you know someone who knows them, or you have a real reason to reach out).

Use this template — open a Google Sheet, name it "Prospects [Your Name]":

| # | Business name | Owner first name | Owner last name | How I know them | Phone / email | Channel I'll use | Status |
|---|---------------|------------------|-----------------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|--------|

Filter by ranking the list:

- **Tier A — best 10**: People you'd happily run into at a coffee shop. They'd take your call today. Almost certainly say yes to a 15-min walkthrough.
- **Tier B — solid 10**: You've done business with them or have a mutual connection. Will take a meeting if your message is sharp.
- **Tier C — stretch 10**: You know they exist, you have a credible angle, but you'd be cold to them.

Work Tier A first. Always.

### Categories to focus on (highest activation rate)

1. **Cafes / coffee shops** — high repeat-customer ratio, low-friction loyalty story
2. **Salons / barbers** — book-of-business already exists, owners get loyalty
3. **Gyms / studios** — already use loyalty/membership tools; LYMX is a complement, not a replacement
4. **Restaurants** (independent, not chains) — strong customer retention pain point
5. **Self-employed pros** (massage therapists, tutors, freelance designers) — book through bookings/sessions

### Categories to avoid (slow, low-fit)

- Auto repair, mechanics — low repeat frequency, hard sell
- Big-box retail — already has loyalty programs
- E-commerce-only businesses — no physical Vegas footprint
- Anything with VC money or franchise structure — long approval chain

## Step 3 — The 15-min business call

Once you've booked a 15-min Zoom with a prospect, here's the structure. Memorize the rough beats; **don't read from a script** — they'll hear it and you'll lose.

### Minute 0–2: Open

> "Thanks for the time. I'll keep this to 15 min. Here's the deal: I joined a thing called LYMX as a Partner. It's a loyalty rewards platform built specifically for small Vegas businesses. I'll walk you through it in 5 minutes, then I want to hear what you think — fit or not fit, totally fine either way. Sound good?"

### Minute 2–7: Show, don't tell

Open the LYMX dashboard demo (Kenny will share the link). Walk through:

1. **The customer view** — "Customers download the app, scan, and earn LYMX rewards every visit."
2. **The business view** — "You see exactly which customers came in, how often, what they spent. Most small businesses have never seen this data."
3. **The network view** — "Every business in the LYMX network brings ~1,500 of their own customers into the network monthly. Customers move between businesses. You get traffic from cafes / gyms / salons that aren't yours."

Do not show pricing in this 5-min stretch. Just show the value.

### Minute 7–10: The pitch

> "Here's how it works. You sign up — three months free, no payment info today, $850 sign-up only after we approve you. After three months, $199/month. You set the LYMX-per-dollar rate when you join — most businesses pick 5 LYMX per $1 spent — and you control your own promotional multipliers. Cancel anytime, 90 days to redeem outstanding LYMX."
>
> "If you're interested, I'd love to be the Partner who brings you on. Not a sales thing — I just personally get $750 + a small recurring share when you join, and it helps me hit my Founding 25 number. **{{personalize: this is my Nth conversation this week / I'm trying to hit 5 in 30 days}}**."

### Minute 10–13: Their questions

Most common questions and how to answer:

**Q: "Is this a Groupon / discount thing?"**
A: No — Groupon is a one-time discount. LYMX is a loyalty platform. Your pricing doesn't change. Customers earn rewards on every visit and redeem when they hit a threshold.

**Q: "How much do my customers actually save?"**
A: At 5 LYMX per $1, customers earn 5% back. So $100 spent = $5 in LYMX. Comparable to most credit-card cashback.

**Q: "Will my customers actually use this?"**
A: Adoption is on you and us. Most LYMX businesses see 30–50% of repeat customers signed up within the first 60 days, especially with the launch email blast we send to local LYMX customers when you join.

**Q: "What if my business has multiple locations?"**
A: $199 base + $50/mo per additional location. LYMX rewards work across all your locations seamlessly.

**Q: "Sounds like an MLM."**
A: Totally fair to ask. There's no buy-in for businesses. You pay $850 only after approval. The Partner program (my side) is similar to a sales rep getting commission — I get paid because I bring you on, but you don't pay me. You pay LYMX directly.

### Minute 13–15: Close

> "So — gut read. Want to move forward, want to think about it, or not a fit?"

If yes: "Great. I'll send you the sign-up link right now. Takes 3 minutes. After you submit, you'll hear back within 1 business day for approval."

If "let me think": "Sure — let me send you the 1-pager and the napkin math. Anything specific I should answer in writing? When should I check back — Friday work?"

If no: "Totally fair. Last ask: do you know one other Vegas business owner who might be open to it? Even just a name and a text — would help me a lot."

## Step 4 — After they sign up

The moment a prospect submits the business application:

1. Text them within 5 minutes: "Got it — application in. Approval comes through tomorrow. I'll text you the moment it lands."
2. Slack-DM Kenny (or whoever's processing approvals): "{{Prospect name + business}} just submitted — please prioritize review."
3. After approval, text the new business: "You're in! I'll be at your location {{day/time}} to help you get the dashboard live. Bring your POS login credentials."

The first 48 hours after approval is when **you earn or lose the business**. If you ghost them and they have to figure the dashboard out alone, they churn before month 3. **Show up in person or on Zoom for the first dashboard configuration.** That's the activation.

## Step 5 — The dashboard configuration

When you sit down with the new business owner:

1. **Sign them in to the dashboard** at lymx.netlify.app/business/dashboard.
2. **Help them set their LYMX-per-dollar rate.** Default is 5 LYMX per $1. Cafes and lower-margin businesses sometimes pick 3 LYMX per $1. Show them the competitor comparison (Square Loyalty, Toast, Yelp) — most charge 8–15% effective return; LYMX at 5% is competitive.
3. **Activate their promotional multipliers.** 2× for first-time customers. 3× on slow days (Tue/Wed for restaurants).
4. **Print and post their LYMX QR code at checkout.** This is the single biggest activation lever — visible QR at the register doubles signup rate.
5. **Send the launch email blast.** Coordinate with Kenny — this is the one-time blast to all local LYMX customers in their metro.

## What kills Partner momentum

Three patterns I've seen kill Partners' first 30 days:

1. **Going wide instead of deep.** Texting 100 cold prospects is worse than texting 10 warm ones. Tier A first, always.
2. **Skipping the dashboard demo.** "I'll just send them the link" — they don't sign up. The 5-min walkthrough is what closes.
3. **Not showing up after sign-up.** The business activates 70% if you sit with them, 25% if they're left to figure it out alone.

---

## What makes Partners win

The Founding 25 Partners I've watched succeed share three things:

1. **They use their own network first.** They don't cold-pitch. They open with "you've known me for years — give me 15 min."
2. **They bring proof.** They show the dashboard, the actual data, not a slide deck.
3. **They follow up within 24 hours.** Every single conversation. No silence.
