The 4 retention principles

  1. Day 30 is the cliff. If a new customer doesn't return by day 30, they probably never will. Focus your retention spend there.
  2. Habits beat promotions. A customer who comes 3 times in their first month is 8x more likely to be a year-long regular than one who comes once.
  3. Network-aware retention works better. Your customers earn LYMX at OTHER Businesses too. Bring them back when they have a balance worth redeeming.
  4. Personalization scales. Saying their name. Remembering their order. The cashier who treats them like a regular. Tech can help, but humans close the loop.

🚩 Retention killers — avoid these

  • Generic mass texts. "10% off this weekend" to everyone is spam. Targeted segments work.
  • Over-messaging. More than 2 messages per month per customer = unsubscribe risk.
  • Discount-led retention. "Come back, 20% off!" trains customers to wait for discounts. LYMX bonuses do the opposite — they give MORE without devaluing your menu.
  • Ignoring complaints. A customer with one bad experience who doesn't get a response = gone forever. Use biz-dispute-handling.
  • Cashier turnover affecting "remember the regular" feel. Train new cashiers on top customers in their first week.
  • Failing to acknowledge top customers. Your top 5 want to know you see them. A 30-second hello goes far.

FAQ

How quickly will retention tactics start showing results?

Tactic 1 (day-30 SMS): immediately, you'll see 20%+ recovery. Tactic 3 (3-visit lock): you'll see 30-day metric improvement within ~2 months. Most retention tactics need 90 days for clear data.

What's the best single retention tactic if I can only pick one?

The day-30 SMS (#1). Highest leverage per minute of setup. ~22% recovery rate is industry-leading.

How do I measure if retention is improving?

Dashboard → Reports → Cohort retention. Tracks each month's new customers and their return rates over time. Compare cohorts month-over-month.

Should I focus on retention or new customer acquisition?

Both. Healthy mix: 30% retention spend, 70% acquisition early on. Once your network density grows, flip to 60% retention, 40% acquisition.

Can my Local Manager run these tactics independently?

Most yes — Local Managers can run promos, send messages, view customer segments at their location. Chain-wide retention strategy stays with the Owner. See biz-staff-roles.
