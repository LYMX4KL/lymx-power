Your first 7 days

A day-by-day playbook for new LYMX Partners. The goal of week 1 is NOT to close a Business — it's to learn the network, complete onboarding, build a target list, and have one real Business meeting scheduled.

The week 1 promise

If you do every day's tasks, by Sunday night you'll have: a complete LYMX product knowledge base, your CRM seeded with 30+ qualified Business prospects, your first Business pitch meeting scheduled, and a feedback session with your mentor. That's the launch pad. Closing comes in weeks 2-4.

1 Monday

Onboarding

Morning (2 hours)

  • Onboarding call with mentor. Your assigned mentor walks you through their first 30 days. Ask everything.
  • LYMX product walk-through. Read what-is-lymx, referral-mechanics, flywheel. 30 minutes.
  • Sign Partner agreement. Read partner-agreement in full. Don't skim.

Afternoon (2 hours)

  • Customer wallet setup. Open your own LYMX wallet at wallet.lymx.com. You can't sell what you haven't used.
  • Visit 3 LYMX Businesses. Make a purchase, scan QR, earn LYMX, redeem at the next one. Live the customer experience.
  • Read partner-faq end to end. 25 minutes.
End of day 1You've got an active LYMX wallet, signed agreement, met your mentor, and earned LYMX at 3 Businesses. You feel the customer side.

2 Tuesday

Sales toolkit

Morning (2 hours)

  • Read partner-discovery-script. The first 6 minutes script. Memorize it.
  • Read partner-pitch-template. The 30-min meeting flow. Bookmark it.
  • Read partner-objections. The top 20. Don't memorize answers — just understand the patterns.

Afternoon (2 hours)

  • Roleplay with mentor. 30-min mock pitch. They play hostile owner. You stumble. That's fine. Take notes on what felt hard.
  • Run revenue calc on 3 sample Businesses. Use biz-revenue-calc. Get fluent with the numbers.
  • Set up your partner-crm. Link to your phone, calendar.
End of day 2You can run discovery + pitch from memory. You've roleplayed once. CRM is live.

3 Wednesday

Target list

Morning (3 hours)

  • Build a 50-Business target list. Drive your assigned territory. Note: name, address, owner-on-premise (yes/no), POS visible, busy/slow.
  • Filter to 30 qualified. Cut: chains (corporate decisions), <90 day open, hostile owner. Keep: independents, owner-operated, 6mo+ open.
  • Prioritize top 10. By: high revenue + high pain + warm in some way (mentor's intro, friend's recommendation, neighbor of existing Business).

Afternoon (1 hour)

  • Research top 10. Who's the owner? How long open? What's their Yelp rating? Any obvious gap?
  • Plan day 4 visits. 5-7 walk-ins for tomorrow morning, in geographic order.
End of day 330+ Businesses in CRM, 10 prioritized for first contact, day 4 schedule planned.

4 Thursday

First conversations

Morning (3 hours)

  • Walk in to your first 5-7 Businesses. Use the discovery script. Don't pitch — just qualify.
  • Take notes after each. Owner name, pain point, qualifier rating, next step. Update CRM same day.

Afternoon (1 hour)

  • Mentor debrief. 20-min call. Walk through each conversation. What surprised you? What got stuck?
  • Plan day 5 visits. Different neighborhoods. Adjust script based on day 4 lessons.
Common day 4 mistakePitching too early. The discovery script says "I'm not here to sell you anything today." If you find yourself launching into LYMX features after 2 minutes, you've broken the script. Reset.

5 Friday

More conversations

Morning (3 hours)

  • Walk in to 5-7 more Businesses. Discovery script again. You're getting smoother.
  • Aim for at least 1 meeting scheduled. Even if it's next week. The discovery → meeting conversion you're building.

Afternoon (1 hour)

  • Update CRM. All conversations from week logged. Pipeline visible.
  • Send follow-up emails to "maybe" Businesses. Templates from partner-followup-templates. Schedule recheck dates.
End of day 5You've talked to 10-14 Business owners. CRM has stages. At least 1 meeting scheduled.

6 Saturday

Reflection + bootcamp

Morning (2 hours)

  • Attend Saturday Partner bootcamp. Live session, see partner-bootcamp. Top Partners share weekly tactics.
  • Note: 2-3 things to try next week. Don't try everything. Pick the highest-leverage.

Afternoon (1 hour)

  • Review your week's metrics. # walk-ins, # discovery conversations, # qualified, # meetings scheduled. See chart below for benchmarks.
  • Patch knowledge gaps. If something came up that you couldn't answer, study it now. Common gaps: 80% redemption rule, asset transfer, multi-location pricing.

7 Sunday

Plan week 2

Morning (2 hours)

  • Long mentor session. Review entire week's CRM with them. They'll catch things you missed.
  • Set week 2 goals. Concrete targets: # meetings, # qualified, first sign-up by day 14.

Afternoon (1 hour)

  • Pre-meeting prep for week 2. Open your scheduled meetings. Build a custom revenue-calc for each.
  • Rest. You'll need it.
End of week 1Onboarding complete. Foundation built. Week 2 set up. You're a real LYMX Partner now.

Common week-1 mistakes

  • Pitching during discovery. The first 6-minute conversation is qualification, not selling. Resist.
  • Not logging conversations. If you didn't enter it in CRM, it didn't happen. You'll forget by Friday.
  • Trying to close in week 1. Most week-1 closes are flukes. Real wins start week 2-3.
  • Hiding from mentor. They've seen everything you're hitting. Use them.
  • Bringing every objection back to "$199 is reasonable." Every Business has a different real concern. Listen first, frame second.
  • Ignoring the "maybe" pile. Maybes become yeses with timing. Schedule rechecks. They compound.
