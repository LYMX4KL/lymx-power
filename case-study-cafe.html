Sarah Chen opened Brew & Bean in 2019 and grew it into one of Midtown's most-loved cafés the slow way — good coffee, friendly staff, and a sunny patio. By 2025 she had two consistent problems: keeping new customers coming back, and knowing whether anything she spent on marketing was actually working.

The setup before LYMX

What Brew & Bean was running

  • $340/mo Yelp Ads — drove some clicks, hard to attribute to actual visits
  • $120/mo Square Loyalty — a stamp card that customers forgot existed
  • $210/mo email tool — sent twice a month to ~800 contacts
  • $80/mo Instagram boosting — occasional
  • Total: $750/mo on customer acquisition + retention

What they were getting

  • ~28 new customers per month from all sources combined
  • Repeat-visit rate of 41% within 90 days (industry avg ~50%)
  • No way to tell which channel drove which customer
  • Stamp card data trapped inside Square — no portability
  • Sarah spent 2–3 hours a week running the email tool herself
"We were spending real money every month and I genuinely could not tell you what any of it was doing. The stamp card felt like the only thing that worked, but it was also the cheapest one — that should have told me something." Sarah Chen, Owner

Why she tried LYMX

A neighbor — Daniel from Oakline Kitchen, two blocks down — joined LYMX in early February 2026 as a Launch 25 Business and mentioned it over coffee. Sarah's hesitation: she'd been pitched loyalty programs before. They all promised acquisition; none of them delivered. The thing that got her over the line was the network effect.

"Daniel said something that flipped it for me — every other LYMX Business has a financial reason to send their customers to me, because their customer's LYMX work here too. It's not a loyalty program. It's a shared customer base." Sarah Chen, Owner

The 8-week rollout

Week 1 · Feb 8, 2026

Sign up + setup

$850 sign-up fee, plus $200/mo recurring. POS replaced with LYMX (kept Square as backup for first month).

Week 2

Cancelled Square Loyalty + Yelp Ads

Sarah paused Square Loyalty (kept Square as POS, just turned off the stamp card). Cancelled Yelp Ads. Cost savings: $460/mo immediately.

Week 3

First "borrowed" customer

A customer redeemed LYMX they'd earned at Oakline Kitchen for a coffee. Sarah didn't pay any acquisition cost for that customer — Oakline had effectively pre-paid it.

Week 4–5

Set up first item-level boost

2× multiplier on cold brew ($5.50, normally 28 LYMX → boosted to 55 LYMX). Cold brew sales lifted 38% in two weeks.

Week 6

Hit break-even on monthly cost

By week 6, the 142 new LYMX customers/month were generating enough incremental margin to cover the $200/mo subscription with $740 left over.

Week 8 · Apr 5, 2026

Switched the email tool off

LYMX's built-in customer engagement (push to wallet, in-app receipts, multiplier promos) replaced the standalone email tool. Cancelled. Cost savings: $210/mo more.

What surprised her

Three things, in her words.

1. The customers from other Businesses showed up faster than expected. By week 3, ~15% of transactions were from customers who'd earned LYMX somewhere else. By week 8, that number was 31%. None of those customers cost Sarah anything to acquire.

2. The boosted items moved more than the math suggested they would. A 2× multiplier on cold brew costs Sarah ~$0.05 in wholesale per drink. Cold brew sales went up 38% in two weeks. The math comes out to roughly $24 in boost cost driving $620 in incremental sales — a 25× return.

3. The data was more useful than the loyalty. Sarah cared about retention, but the part she ended up using daily was the dashboard — being able to see which times of day generated the most LYMX transactions, which menu items had the lowest attach, which days were softer than the network average. That's not what she signed up for, but it's what kept her engaged.

"I thought I was buying a loyalty program. What I actually got was a shared customer pipeline plus the analytics tool I should have had years ago." Sarah Chen, Owner

What's next for Brew & Bean

Sarah is now hosting a monthly "LYMX Latte Hour" event for other small Business owners in Midtown — informal Q&A sessions for people considering joining. She's also become a Partner — earning override commission on the 4 Businesses she's referred to date. Her first month as a Partner: $740 in commission alongside her own Business income.
