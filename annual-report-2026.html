Section 1

A letter from the founder.

To our customers, our Businesses, our Partners, our team, and our investors —

Year One is done. As I write this in late December 2026, LYMX serves 218 active Businesses, 14,820 active customers, and operates in two cities (Las Vegas and Phoenix). We've grown from an idea I'd been carrying around for two years to a working network with measurable economic impact.

The thing I keep returning to is this: when small Businesses pool their loyalty, the math gets better for everyone. The customer earning at Brew & Bean and redeeming at the bookstore — that customer is supported by a network that no single Business could build alone. That's the thesis. After 12 months of operation, the data is consistent with the thesis, but I want to be careful about declaring victory. Network businesses look like they're working until they aren't. We're at the start of the curve, not the end.

What worked in 2026: the Founding 25 program (we filled it in 18 days and 100% are still active). The Square + Toast integrations (we shipped both within the first 6 months). The Partner program (60+ active Partners now driving meaningful Business acquisition). The 90-day customer cohort retention curve (84% → 91% → 94%, exceeding our model).

What didn't go the way I expected: Our Phoenix launch took longer than planned (originally Q3, actually ended up Q4 because we wanted to apply learnings from Vegas before opening). Two Founding-25 prospects backed out at the last minute, requiring me to recruit replacements personally. Our Toast integration's beta period went 6 weeks longer than scoped due to webhook reliability issues we hadn't anticipated. None of these were existential — but they cost time and a little credibility.

What I'm proudest of: We didn't compromise on the redemption model. The temptation when you're starting was real — investors asked about variable redemption rates, Businesses asked about per-store overrides. We held the line: 1 LYMX = $0.01, network-wide, fixed. The discipline matters more in year one than it will in year ten.

Looking at 2027: We're targeting 4 cities live by mid-year, $5M in committed network volume, and Series A funding by Q3. Every milestone is a question, not an answer. We'll find out together.

Thank you for being part of this.

Kenny Lin
Founder & CEO, LYMX Power
December 28, 2026 · Las Vegas, NV

Product priorities

The 2027 product roadmap focuses on three threads: multi-city customer experience (cross-city redemption, regional promo support), Business analytics depth (cohort tracking, automated insights, predictive multiplier ROI), and API ecosystem (more integrations, official Partner SDK, white-label tools).

Detailed roadmap available at getlymx.com/roadmap with public voting on what we ship next.

Risks we're watching

Three things we worry about, in honest order: (1) Phoenix density — needs 50+ Businesses by mid-2027 to hit the network-effect threshold. We're behind on that. (2) Square or Toast launching a competing offering — possible but slow given their decision cycles. (3) Capital markets — Series A timing depends on broader fundraising environment. Plan B: extend runway via insider bridge from existing investors.
