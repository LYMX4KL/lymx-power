# Friends & Family Outreach
**A separate, warmer track from the public campaign.**

---

## Why this is different from the cold campaign

Friends and family already know you. They don't need to be pitched on the math the way a cold contact does. What they need is:

1. To know that **you're doing something serious** and what it is in plain language.
2. To know **exactly what you're asking of them** — even if it's just "share this link" or "intro me to one person."
3. A way to **forward** the message to others without it sounding like an MLM recruit.

So the tone here is conversational, not campaign-y. No "FOUNDING 25" branding. No urgency math. No "DM me, link in bio." Just you, talking to someone who already cares about you.

The flip side: most of these conversations should still **end with a specific ask.** Your friends and family want to help you — but they need to know how. Vague "let me know if you think of anyone" is a wasted message. "Would you intro me to your sister-in-law who runs the salon?" is a real ask.

**Three asks, ranked by lift:**

| Ask | Conversion | Effort for them |
|-----|-----------|-----------------|
| **Become a Partner themselves** | Highest lift (they already trust you) | Highest |
| **Intro you to one specific business owner they know** | Very high lift (warm intros close near 100%) | Low |
| **Sign their own business up if they own one** | High lift (1 Direct closer to qualifying) | Medium |
| **Share the campaign on social / forward to peers** | Useful background buzz | Lowest |

Plan to make Ask #2 (specific intro) the default. It's the lowest-friction high-lift ask.

## 1. The first message — close family / closest friends

Send this to your **inner circle of 10-15 people** before anything else this week. Ideally Sunday May 10 evening, **before the public launch on Monday morning** — so they hear it from you first.

**Channel:** Text or iMessage. Not email. Not Facebook. Definitely not LinkedIn.

### Template

> Hey {{name}} —
>
> Heads up: tomorrow I'm officially launching the Vegas push for LYMX. The thing I've been building. Wanted you to hear it from me first before you see it on LinkedIn / IG.
>
> The next 4 weeks I'm looking for 25 Partners in Vegas. People who can help me bring local businesses onto the platform. The first 25 to qualify lock founding perks for life — $750 cash per business they bring on (paid up front), plus 11% of every business's monthly subscription, recurring forever. Hit 5 in 30 days and there's a $1,000 speed bonus + featured spotlight.
>
> Two ways you can help if you want:
>
> 1) Know one Vegas business owner who might want to try LYMX? Intro me — even just a name + their business, I'll take it from there.
>
> 2) Know one Vegas-based person who's good at sales / has a small-business network? Send them my way. They'd be a great Partner candidate.
>
> No pressure either way. Just appreciate you being in my corner. Will keep you posted.
>
> — K

### Customization

- **For close family:** lean even warmer. Tell them you'll show them the dashboard the next time you see them. Add: "I'll loop you in when we hit the first 5 Partners."
- **For close friends with their own business:** add a line — "If you'd ever consider listing {{their business}} on LYMX as one of the first activations, I'd love to walk you through it." That's Ask #3.
- **For close friends who are sales pros / network builders:** lean into Ask #1 — "Honestly, you'd be a great Partner yourself. I think you'd kill it." Then offer the calendly link.

## 2. The wider circle — extended friends, cousins, college, work alumni

Send this Tuesday May 12 (after the public launch is up, so they have a LinkedIn post to react to if they want context).

**Channel:** Text first, but Instagram DM works for people you mostly interact with on IG.

### Template

> Hey — random update. I'm spending the next month finding 25 Partners in Vegas for LYMX, the loyalty platform I've been working on. The first 25 to qualify lock permanent founding perks ($750 cash per business they bring on, plus 11% recurring on every monthly subscription, plus a $1,000 bonus if they hit 5 activations in 30 days).
>
> If you happen to know **one Vegas small-business owner** — cafe, salon, gym, freelancer, whoever — who might want a free 3-month trial on LYMX, would you intro me? Even a quick text saying "you should talk to my friend Kenny" is a huge unlock for me.
>
> Otherwise no pressure, just wanted to let you know what I'm up to. Hope you're well.
>
> — K

### When to follow up

If no reply after 5 days, send a low-pressure check-in:

> No worries if it slipped — was there one biz owner who came to mind? If not all good. Just figured worth one more try while the founding window is open.

If still no reply after that, drop it. Don't chase three times.

## 3. The "forward this to one person" message

Use this when someone says "I'd love to help — what can I do?" but doesn't have a specific intro top-of-mind. Give them something easy to forward.

### Template (paste this for them — make it copyable)

> Forward this to one Vegas business owner you know:
>
> ---
>
> *"Friend of mine just launched a loyalty rewards platform for small Vegas businesses called LYMX. 3 months free, no payment info up front, $850 sign-up bonus paid after approval. He's looking for 100 Vegas businesses to be on board by June 7. If your business takes repeat customers, worth a 15-min look. Here's the page: lymx.netlify.app/business — and his name is Kenny if you DM."*

This makes it **trivially easy** for your friend to help. They literally copy, paste, and send. Lower the activation energy and people will actually do it.

## 4. The "Sunday family dinner" version

Some of your strongest advocates will want to ask questions in person. Sunday family dinners, BBQs, etc. They'll ask "so what is this thing?" — and if you don't have a 30-second answer, the conversation dies.

### 30-second elevator (memorize this verbatim)

> "I built a loyalty rewards platform for small Vegas businesses — like the kind of customer-rewards data Starbucks has, but for the cafe down the street. Businesses sign up, customers earn rewards on every visit, and the businesses finally see who their repeat customers are. Three months free, no upfront cost. I'm looking for 25 Partners across Vegas to help bring businesses on board — and the first 25 lock permanent founding perks. Recurring income, no fees forever. We're four weeks into a sprint right now."

Practice saying it out loud three times in a row, casually, until it doesn't sound like a script.

### Then the natural follow-up question is "how can I help?"

Always have one of three answers ready:

1. **If they own a Vegas business:** "If you'd let me set you up as one of my early activations, that would be huge."
2. **If they have a network of business owners:** "If anyone comes to mind, intro me — even just a name and a text."
3. **If neither:** "Honestly, the biggest thing is share the LinkedIn post. Even a like makes the algorithm push it to your network."

## 5. The "sorry I can't" deflection

You're going to get the "Wait, is this an MLM?" question from someone in your circle. Probably more than once. Have a clean answer ready.

### Template (~60 words)

> Totally fair to ask. The short answer: no. There's no buy-in, no starter kit, no "sponsor above you." LYMX is a real product — software for small businesses to track their loyalty rewards, like Square does for payments. Partners earn a recurring share when the businesses they bring on subscribe. That's it. You don't pay to join, you don't recruit other Partners under you, and the comp is from the business subscriptions, not from other Partners' fees.

If they keep pushing, end the conversation gracefully:

> Look — I get it, this kind of thing burns people. If it doesn't smell right to you, that's totally fine. The offer's open if you ever want to look at the actual product. No hard feelings either way.

Then **drop it** and move on. Don't argue. Family especially — being right is less important than the relationship.

## 6. The "thank-you" loop

Every person who actually does something for you — sends a referral, signs their business up, posts on social — gets a **specific, personalized thank-you** within 48 hours.

### Template

> {{name}} — just wanted to say thank you for {{specific action: "introducing me to Maria at the salon" / "sharing my LinkedIn post" / "signing up Tony's Pizza last night"}}. It actually moved the needle. I owe you {{coffee / lunch / a drink — pick one and mean it}}. Will follow up.
>
> — K

Then **actually follow through** on the thank-you. Take them to coffee. Buy the drink. The campaign is short — 4 weeks — but your relationships are forever, and people remember who said thanks and who didn't.

## 7. The "after the campaign" message

When you hit your number (or don't), send this to everyone in your friends-and-family circle on Mon June 8.

### If you hit 50 Partners

> Quick update. We did it. 50 Partners signed up across Vegas in 4 weeks. 5 of them already qualified for Founding 25. Couldn't have gotten here without {{specific person / family / friend group}}. More to come — keeping you in the loop.
>
> Thank you. — K

### If you hit something less than 50

> Quick update. Closed the 4-week sprint with {{N}} Partners. Less than I aimed for, but more than I would've gotten without your help — particularly {{specific person who helped}}. The Founding window is now closed but the 25 race continues into June.
>
> Will keep going. Thank you for being in my corner.
>
> — K

Either way, **send this to everyone**. The people who didn't help still want to know how it went. And the next time you launch something, they'll be more likely to engage early because you closed the loop.
