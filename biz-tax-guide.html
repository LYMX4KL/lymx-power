Most LYMX Businesses use cash-basis accounting

If you're under $25M in revenue (which is most LYMX Businesses), you can use cash-basis accounting. Translation: record income when you receive cash and expenses when you pay them. Simpler than accrual.

LYMX redemption cost — COGS or marketing?

This is the question your accountant will ask. When LYMX you issued is redeemed at another Business, you owe 80% of the face value at settlement (the remaining 20% is the network's automatic referral discount you "give" the customer). That 80% expense needs a category. There are two valid approaches:

Option A: COGS (cost of goods sold)

Treat LYMX issuance as a cost of generating each sale. This works well if you can reasonably attribute the LYMX cost to specific transactions (which LYMX makes easy via settlement reports).

Pros: Lowers gross profit margin (matches reality). Cleanly attributes cost to revenue.
Cons: Requires per-transaction tracking. Slightly more bookkeeping work.

Option B: Operating expense (marketing/advertising)

Treat LYMX issuance as a marketing expense. Easier to record because you can take it from monthly settlement totals.

Pros: Simpler. One number per month from your settlement report.
Cons: Doesn't match cost-to-revenue as cleanly. Inflates marketing expense line.

💡

Most LYMX Businesses use Option B

Treating it as a marketing expense is simpler and our settlement reports support it. Talk to your accountant about which is right for your situation. Once you pick, stay consistent.

$850 sign-up & $199/mo subscription

The $850 sign-up fee is a one-time cost. You have two valid choices for accounting:

  1. Capitalize and amortize the $850 over the expected useful life (often 12 months → $70/mo). This smooths the expense over time.
  2. Expense it fully in the month paid as a marketing or technology expense. Simpler. Most small Businesses do this.

The $199/mo subscription is straightforwardly a recurring operating expense. Categorize as "Software" or "Marketing" — either is acceptable. Keep your categorization consistent month over month.

📊

Reconciliation tip

Your monthly LYMX-related expense is: (Subscription) + (Net wholesale settlement, if positive — meaning you issued more than was redeemed). If your net settlement is negative (more redeemed than issued), you actually got money back — record as income reduction, not extra revenue.

Deductible LYMX-related expenses

Beyond the obvious, here are LYMX-specific costs you should track:

📥Subscription & sign-up

Recurring $199/mo + one-time $850. Both fully deductible as operating or marketing expenses.

💰Wholesale rate (LYMX issued)

$0.008 × number of LYMX issued. Treat as COGS or marketing per Option A or B above.

🖨️Print kit & signage

Window clings, table tents, register cards. Marketing expense — fully deductible.

📱POS hardware (if new)

Tablets, card readers, scanners purchased for LYMX setup. Section 179 deductible up to $1.16M (2026 limit) — usually full deduction in year purchased.

🎓Training time (if you're an employee owner)

Time spent training staff on LYMX. Doesn't directly deduct unless you're paying yourself — but worth tracking for time-allocation purposes.

🎁Customer giveaway promotions

If you run a "$5 free LYMX for every customer" promo, the wholesale cost of those issued LYMX is a deductible marketing expense.

Bookkeeping flow — month by month

Here's the rhythm we recommend. Takes 15 minutes per month if your books are otherwise organized.

1st of the month — receive settlement report

Settlement report email arrives in your inbox at 8 AM PT. Forward to your accountant or upload to QuickBooks. Note the net settlement amount.

5th of the month — net settlement clears

ACH debit or credit hits your bank. Match it to the corresponding line in your accounting software. The transaction memo should auto-include "LYMX-Settlement-YYYY-MM".

Same day — categorize

Categorize the LYMX wholesale portion as COGS or Marketing (your Option A or B choice). Categorize the $200 subscription as Software or Marketing.

End of quarter — review

Run a 90-day report from your LYMX dashboard. Compare to your accounting software's LYMX-categorized expenses. They should match within 1–2%. If they don't, contact support — you may have categorization errors.

Year-end — pull the annual settlement summary

In your dashboard, go to Settlement → Annual Summary. This is the document your CPA will use for tax filing. Available January 15 of the following year.

FAQ

Are LYMX redemptions taxable as income to me?

No. When a customer redeems LYMX at your Business, treat it like a discount applied at checkout, not separate revenue. Your gross sales are the customer's full purchase price; the LYMX redemption is a discount. Your accounting income is the post-discount amount.

Do I need to issue a 1099 to LYMX?

No. We're a service provider, not a contractor. We issue 1099-K to YOU (if your transaction volume exceeds the threshold), not the other way around.

I cancelled LYMX mid-year. How do I handle the unused subscription?

If you cancel mid-month, your final invoice is pro-rated. The expense is whatever you actually paid. There's no "carry-forward" of unused subscription value.

Do I get a refund on the $850 if I cancel?

Per our refund policy, the $850 is partially refundable within the first 30 days. After that, no refund — but you can pause for up to 60 days without losing your spot. Talk to support before cancelling.

What if I get audited?

Your monthly settlement reports + dashboard exports are sufficient documentation for a tax audit. We retain 7 years of your transaction history. If you need historical settlement data, contact support — we can pull anything from your account.

Sales tax — does LYMX issuance affect that?

No. Sales tax is calculated on the customer's gross purchase price. LYMX issuance happens after the sale and doesn't change taxable revenue. Sales tax compliance is your responsibility (Square / Toast usually handle this for you).
