Your privacy rights at LYMX

Plain-English version of what data LYMX has on you, what rights you have over it, and the actual buttons to click. We don't sell customer data — full stop. Below is the detail.

The LYMX privacy pledge

1. We collect the minimum needed to run your wallet — phone, name, transactions.

2. We don't sell your data. Period. Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to Businesses on the network.

3. Businesses on the network see ANONYMIZED activity at their location only, never your phone, email, or name.

4. You can export, delete, or correct your data at any time, free, no questions asked.

What data we have on you

✅ What we collect

  • Phone numberThe unique identifier for your wallet.
  • First name onlyFor greeting at register. Last name optional.
  • Transaction historyEvery earn + redemption, with Business + amount + date.
  • Birthday (month + day, no year) — if you opt inFor birthday bonus promos. Optional.
  • Approximate cityDerived from your IP at sign-up. Used for "Businesses near you" feature.
  • Device + browser typeFor technical support + fraud prevention.

❌ What we DON'T collect

  • Last name (unless you provide)Optional, never required.
  • Email addressNot asked at sign-up.
  • Home addressNever collected.
  • Birth yearMonth/day only.
  • Race, gender, demographicsNever collected.
  • Credit card detailsCard data stays with the Business' POS, never touches LYMX.
  • Browsing history outside LYMXWe don't track you across the web.
  • Location after sign-upNot tracked unless you opt in to "Find Businesses near me."
  • Contacts / address bookNever accessed.
  • Social media accountsNot linked unless you choose to.

Common scenarios

"A Business asked me for my email — can I just use my LYMX wallet instead?"

LYMX doesn't share your email or phone with Businesses. The Business sees your activity at their location only — not your contact info.

Tell the Business: "I'm on LYMX, scan my QR." That's all they need to credit your purchase. No email required.

"Can a Business email me directly using their LYMX customer list?"

No — Businesses don't have your email or phone. They can send you messages through the LYMX wallet (we deliver, you receive in-app).

If you want to opt out of those messages: Wallet → Settings → Privacy → "Receive Business messages" off.

"How do I know what Businesses I've been at?"

Wallet → History tab. Filterable by date, by Business. Or export the full thing for any time period.

Go to customer-history.html for the full breakdown including by-Business roll-ups.

"I want to delete one specific transaction from my history."

You can hide it from your view, but the underlying record stays for accounting/legal compliance. Once a transaction has happened, it's permanent — like a credit card transaction.

If you suspect fraud, file a dispute (customer-dispute.html) which can void the transaction.

"What happens to my data if LYMX gets acquired or shuts down?"

Either way: your data rights persist. If acquired, the new company inherits these obligations by contract. If shutdown, all customer data is purged within 90 days of wind-down. You're notified at least 30 days before either event.

See trust-and-safety.html wind-down section for details.

"Am I covered by CCPA / GDPR / state laws?"

The rights above apply to all LYMX customers regardless of jurisdiction. We hold ourselves to the strictest applicable standard. If you're in California (CCPA), the EU (GDPR), or another state with privacy law, you have those statutory rights AND the LYMX rights — whichever is broader.

Specific statutory request? Email privacy@lymx.com with the law you're invoking and we'll process per that statute's requirements.

FAQ

Does LYMX share data with law enforcement?

Only with valid subpoena or court order. We require legal process — not a casual request. We notify you when we receive a subpoena unless legally prohibited.

What about advertising and tracking pixels?

LYMX wallet pages have NO third-party tracking pixels. Our marketing pages have minimal analytics (page-views, no PII). We don't use Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, or similar tracking.

Can my employer see my LYMX activity?

No — your activity is private. We don't share with anyone except the Business where you transacted (and they see only their own transactions with you, anonymized except first name).

What if I want to revoke a Business' access to my data?

Each Business sees their own historical transactions with you — that's part of normal commerce, like a credit card statement. You can't revoke prior transactions. Going forward: don't shop there, no new data is collected.

How long does LYMX keep my data?

Active wallets: indefinitely (until deletion). Deleted wallets: PII purged within 7 days; anonymized transaction records kept up to 7 years for legal/tax compliance.

Can I download my data in a way that I can import into another loyalty program?

JSON export contains the full schema. Whether another loyalty program can import it depends on their format support. We don't lock you in.
