Week 2

Find the rhythm. The first 7 days were the launch. Now you're calibrating.

Tasks for week 2

Daily 5-min dashboard check

Login each morning. Check yesterday's transactions, sign-ups, redemptions. Watch for anomalies.

Cashier 1:1 check-in

Talk to each cashier individually. What's confusing? What's working? Quick fix anything that comes up.

Reply to social comments

Customers asking about LYMX in IG/FB comments. Answer promptly — they're future LYMX members.

First customer-feedback review

Check the 3-5 customers who've signed up. What did they say? Anyone confused? Reach out personally.

Don't run a promo yet

Resist. You need a baseline before you can measure if a promo works. Wait until week 3.

📊 Metrics to watch in week 2

Daily LYMX transactions Steady 8–12+ If declining, signage or cashier flow is broken
New wallet sign-ups / day 2–5 Most come from in-store signage
First redemption? Should appear by day 4–7 First-redemption customers usually came from the network
Cashier confusion rate < 5% Track cashier "I'm not sure" moments
⚠️ Common week-2 issue: Slow Day 8–10 Adoption typically dips between days 7-14 — the easy customers signed up week 1, the resistant ones haven't yet. Don't panic. Trust the trajectory. By week 3, the dip resolves.
💡
Tip: Send a "thanks for being early" email to your first 25 LYMX customers. They're your evangelists. Email template here.

Week 3

First multiplier promo. Light experiments.

Tasks for week 3

Set your first multiplier promo

2× on a single bundle item, or 2× on slow days. Don't go bigger — small experiments first. Set up here.

Announce the promo on social

Use the marketing kit "Multiplier promo" template. Pin to IG profile for the duration.

Watch the promo lift

Compare daily LYMX transactions on promo days vs. non-promo days. Document the lift.

First weekly review with team

30 min team standup. What's working? What's not? Open the dashboard together.

Address any cashier inconsistency

By week 3 you should know if one cashier is doing it differently. Coach individually.

📊 Metrics to watch in week 3

Promo lift (transactions on promo day) +15–40% Below 15% = promo isn't compelling. Above 40% = great hit, scale it
Cross-Business redemptions 5–15% of transactions If > 15%, the network mechanic is working strongly for you
Daily new sign-ups 2–6 Should be slightly higher with the promo running
Cashier flow speed < 5 sec extra per transaction If LYMX is slowing your line, fix cashier flow
⚠️ Common week-3 issue: Over-aggressive promo Some operators run a 5× multiplier in week 3 to "make a splash." Don't. The math is brutal — at 5× you're paying 20¢ per dollar of customer spend. Better to start with 2×, prove it works, scale up later.
📈
Tip: Use the benchmarks page to compare your week-3 numbers to peer Businesses. Two weeks of data is enough to start seeing your position.

Week 4

Review the month. Plan ahead.

Tasks for week 4

Run a full 30-day review (60 min)

Block the time. Open analytics. Walk through everything. What worked? What didn't?

Review your first settlement (mid-month)

You'll receive your first 30-day settlement report on day 30. Reconcile it with your books. Confirm wholesale charges look right.

Compare to peer benchmarks

30 days of data is meaningful. Compare to peers in your category. Find your top-1, top-2, top-3 metrics — and bottom 3.

Decide what to phase out

Old loyalty program (Square Loyalty? Punch cards?). Yelp Ads? Email blasting tool? If LYMX is performing, retire one of these.

Plan month 2's promotions

Use the holiday playbook to map out month 2's promo calendar. Schedule them in advance.

Set a recurring weekly 30-min review

The most successful operators do this every week. Calendar block it now.

📊 30-day metrics — where you should be

Active LYMX customers 200–500 (avg café) Higher for restaurants and high-traffic Businesses
% of transactions with LYMX 35–55% If < 25%, signage or cashier flow needs attention
Cross-Business redemption % 10–25% Network effect is starting to show
Net incremental revenue Positive Subtract LYMX costs from incremental sales lift
NPS +40 or higher In-app NPS prompts give you live data
⚠️ Common week-4 issue: "It's working but not as much as I expected" Reset expectations. Week 4 is mid-curve, not endgame. Network effects compound — most peers see their biggest lifts in months 2–3 as cross-Business redemption density grows. Trust the trajectory. Talk to a coach if you're worried.
