The 20 objections you'll hear

Field-tested by Sienna Vega and our top 10 Partners over 218 Business sign-ups. Each objection includes the actual reasoning behind it, the response that works, and the follow-up move.

All 20 Cost Technical Value Competitive Trust

1 "$199 a month is too expensive for my Business"

Cost
What they actually mean: "I don't see how this pays for itself."
Response"Totally fair. Let me ask — how much do you spend on advertising right now? Yelp ads, Instagram boosts, Google Local? Most Businesses I work with are spending $500-1,500/month on ads that aren't measurable. LYMX is $199/month and it gives you the local network of 8,400+ Vegas customers actively earning at LYMX Businesses. Want me to show you the ROI calculator with your actual numbers?"
Follow-up: Open biz-revenue-calc. Plug in their monthly volume. Most Vegas restaurants break even at ~$5K incremental monthly revenue, which is achievable from 1 promo per month.

2 "Plus the 80% redemption cost? That's even worse"

Cost
What they actually mean: "I think I'm paying for other Businesses' rewards."
Response"You're right that there's an 80% redemption cost when LYMX issued elsewhere is redeemed at your Business. But here's the math: if a customer earned $10 of LYMX at a coffee shop and spends it at your restaurant, you pay $8 (80%) but you got an incremental $50 ticket from a customer who came specifically to redeem. Customers spend 5-10x their LYMX redemption — your incremental revenue is the rest of the order."
Follow-up: Show them biz-tax-guide section on 80% redemption with worked examples.

3 "Why do I have to pay $850 to sign up?"

Cost
What they actually mean: "What am I getting for that one-time fee?"
Response"$850 includes 3 months free service ($597 value), POS integration, your launch marketing kit, signage, a featured spot on our homepage, and an email blast to all local LYMX customers announcing you've joined. So net it's $253 for a launch package that includes onboarding 1,500+ existing customers to your Business."
Follow-up: Walk through what's included on business.html sign-up page.

4 "I just got my POS dialed in, I'm not adding another system"

Tech
What they actually mean: "I can't deal with another tech project."
Response"That's exactly why we integrate directly with your POS. If you're on Square, Toast, Clover, Lightspeed, or Shopify, LYMX is a flip-of-a-switch — about 8 minutes from clicking install to live. No new device, no extra screen for cashiers, no re-training. Want me to show you the actual integration screen for your POS?"
Follow-up: Open biz-pos-comparison. Show them their specific POS page (e.g., biz-integration-square).

5 "My POS isn't on your list"

Tech
What they actually mean: Real blocker if true.
Response"Got it. We support 5 major POS platforms today — Square, Toast, Clover, Lightspeed, Shopify. If you're on something else, we have an API that works with most modern POS, and we can have you live in 2-3 weeks. What POS are you on?"
Follow-up: If they're on a major one we don't support yet (Aloha, Micros), flag to your success coach as a roadmap item. If they're on something obscure, recommend tablet-based fallback.

6 "How do I know it won't slow down my POS?"

Tech
What they actually mean: "I can't have anything that slows down my line."
Response"Fair concern. The LYMX scan adds about 2-3 seconds per transaction — and only when the customer's already pulled out their phone. We've measured this across 218 Businesses. If anything, customers say it's faster than the punch-card alternative because there's no fumbling for the right card."
Follow-up: Reference benchmarks page if needed.

7 "I have a punch card, that's enough loyalty"

Value
What they actually mean: "I don't see what I'm missing."
Response"Punch cards work great for repeat customers — 'come back 10 times, get a free coffee.' Where they fall down is acquisition. LYMX gives you both: your existing repeat customers earn LYMX (just like punch cards), AND new customers who earned LYMX at a different Business have a reason to walk into yours. You're plugging into a network of 8,400 active customers."
Follow-up: Show referral-mechanics — the cross-redemption flywheel.

8 "My customers don't care about loyalty programs"

Value
What they actually mean: "I've tried things that didn't work."
Response"What did you try? [Listen.] Right — and most loyalty programs ARE useless because they require an app download, an account, an email. LYMX is different — the customer doesn't sign up with you, they sign up with the network. So your customer doesn't have to download anything OR commit to your Business. They just earn. That's why our customer adoption is 3-4x higher than typical app-based loyalty."
Follow-up: Show customer-stories — real customers explain why they use LYMX.

9 "I don't have time to manage promos"

Value
What they actually mean: "I can't add another to-do."
Response"You don't have to. The default LYMX setup runs itself — every customer earns LYMX on every purchase, no extra effort. Promos are optional. Most Businesses run zero promos and still see 12-18% lift just from the network effect. If you want to grow faster, we have an annual promo planner you can use as a template — but baseline mode is hands-off."
Follow-up: Reference biz-promo-planner.

10 "What if I sign up and customers don't use it?"

Value
What they actually mean: "What's my downside?"
Response"Two things. First — the network is already 8,400 active customers. So they ARE using it, just at other Businesses currently. When you join, they'll start using it at yours too. Second — we have a 90-day money-back guarantee on the $850 sign-up fee. If LYMX doesn't drive at least 50 unique LYMX customers to your Business in 90 days, we refund the $850."
Follow-up: Reference refund-policy.

11 "How is this different from Toast Loyalty?"

Compete
What they actually mean: "Sell me on the difference."
Response"Toast Loyalty (or Square Loyalty, Clover Loyalty) is closed-loop — your Business' customers earn at your Business and only your Business. LYMX is open-loop — customers earn at YOUR Business AND can spend across the entire network. The flywheel: every other LYMX Business is sending customers to you, and you're sending customers to them."
Follow-up: Show compare-toast deep-dive.

12 "I already have Yelp ads / Google ads"

Compete
What they actually mean: "I have a marketing budget allocated."
Response"They serve different needs. Yelp/Google ads are top-of-funnel — get strangers to your door. LYMX is retention + cross-promotion — get past customers back, and tap into the LYMX customer base. They stack. Most LYMX Businesses still run ads, just smarter ads (e.g., 'we accept LYMX' as a hook in their Yelp ad)."
Follow-up: Don't ask them to ditch their existing marketing. Position LYMX as additive.

13 "Square's loyalty is free with my POS — why pay you?"

Compete
What they actually mean: "$199 vs $0 sounds bad."
Response"Square's basic loyalty is free, you're right. But it's closed-loop — your customers, your Business, no network effect. LYMX adds the network: 8,400+ customers actively spending across 218 Businesses. The $199 buys you access to those customers. You can run BOTH side by side — most Businesses do."
Follow-up: Confirm they can keep their Square loyalty active alongside LYMX. They're not competing, they're complementary.

14 "I've never heard of LYMX, are you legit?"

Trust
What they actually mean: "I'm worried about being scammed."
Response"Totally fair. We launched in Vegas in Q3 2025. We're a Las Vegas company — founded by Kenny Lin, who's owned 12+ restaurants himself. We have 218 active Businesses including [name 3 well-known ones near them]. Want me to introduce you to one of them so you can hear directly?"
Follow-up: Always have 3 reference Businesses ready, ideally in their neighborhood. customer-stories for proof.

15 "What happens to my data if you go out of business?"

Trust
What they actually mean: "I want an exit plan."
Response"Two protections. First — your Business' transaction data is exportable anytime via our data export tool. You own it. Second — if LYMX shuts down, all customer LYMX balances become redeemable as a final settlement. The Business agreement has a 'wind-down' clause that protects everyone in the network. We also have D&O insurance and a financial reserve."
Follow-up: trust-and-safety + business-agreement.

16 "What if a customer commits fraud against me?"

Trust
What they actually mean: "Who absorbs the loss?"
Response"LYMX absorbs verified fraud — you don't pay. Our dispute system has a 0.4% network rate, mostly resolved through retroactive credits. For confirmed fraud (stolen wallet, attribution error), LYMX takes the hit and pursues the fraudster. You're not out of pocket."
Follow-up: biz-dispute-handling.

17 "What if I want to leave the network later?"

Trust
What they actually mean: "Am I locked in?"
Response"No lock-in. Cancel anytime — initiates a 90-day wind-down where you stop issuing LYMX but continue accepting it (so customers aren't stranded). After 90 days you're fully out. Outstanding LYMX you issued is auto-capped to your settlement reserve."
Follow-up: refund-policy section 5b.

18 "Are you about to be acquired by someone bigger?"

Trust
What they actually mean: "I want continuity."
Response"Not on the radar. We're privately owned, profitable, growing. Even if we did get acquired down the road, your Business contract carries forward. Your data, your customer relationships, your LYMX rate — all preserved by contract."
Follow-up: Don't speculate about acquisitions. Stay grounded in what's true today.

19 "I want to think about it"

Trust
What they actually mean: Soft "no" or genuine consideration. Find out which.
Response"Sure — what would help you decide? Specific numbers, references, a demo, or just a couple days to sit with it? I'm happy to set up any of those. What sounds best?"
Follow-up: Don't accept "I'll think about it" without a concrete next step. Pin them down to a specific follow-up date or ask, or you'll lose them.

20 "Send me an email and I'll review it"

Trust
What they actually mean: Polite brush-off. 90% never read it.
Response"Of course. Quick question first — what's the one thing that would have to be true for you to say yes? I want to make sure my email actually addresses that, not just the generic pitch."
Follow-up: If they answer the question, you've revealed the real objection — go back to the relevant playbook above. If they refuse to answer: send the email but don't expect to hear back. Move on.
