Customer support

Inbound support tickets via admin-tickets. Auto-categorized by AI; humans always reply.

SOP-01: New ticket triageDaily · within 1hr

  1. Open admin-tickets. Sort by Open + by oldest first.
  2. For each ticket: read it. Check the customer's wallet history if relevant.
  3. Categorize: customer / Business / Partner / press / investor / fraud-suspected.
  4. Pick a canned reply from admin-emails if applicable. Always personalize the first line.
  5. Set expected resolution time. SLA: 1 business day for standard tickets.
  6. If fraud-suspected, escalate to SOP-08 immediately.
Escalate to Kenny if: press inquiry · investor · suspected lawsuit · > $200 in dispute · customer threatens public complaint

SOP-02: First-time-customer confused about LYMX valueCommon · 5 min reply

  1. Use the canned customer_first_redemption template from admin-emails.
  2. Confirm: 1 LYMX = $0.01, never expires, spendable at any LYMX Business.
  3. Link them to how to redeem at a Business.
  4. If they still don't get it after 2 emails, schedule a 5-min call with Sam.

Settlement disputes

Business filed a dispute via settlement-dispute. SLA: 1-2 business days.

SOP-03: Process incoming settlement dispute1-2 BD

  1. Pull the dispute from the admin-tickets queue. Read the Business's description.
  2. Pull the underlying transaction from the database: SELECT * FROM transactions WHERE id = ...
  3. Compare: what the Business reports vs. what the POS logged vs. what the customer's wallet shows.
  4. If our error → credit the Business immediately, send template dispute_resolved_our_error, close ticket.
  5. If Business error (rare) → reply with the audit trail, ask for clarification before closing.
  6. If customer-side dispute (rarer) → loop in the customer per SOP-09.
  7. If unable to determine within 24h, escalate to Kenny.
Escalate to Kenny: any dispute > 5,000 LYMX · any pattern of disputes from same Business in < 7 days · any dispute that touches the buy/sell mechanic itself.

SOP-04: Settlement statement discrepancy from BusinessMonthly

  1. Business contacts saying "my statement doesn't match my POS log."
  2. Pull both sides — our settlement service log + their POS export.
  3. Common causes: tip-aware accrual mismatch, manual void within 60s, promo multiplier timing.
  4. Walk Business through reconciliation. Always do this on a video call if > 50 LYMX off.
  5. If the issue is on our side, credit + post a public note in incident-history if affecting > 1 Business.

Fraud & abuse

Suspicious account activity, suspected fake accounts, abuse of the referral program.

SOP-05: Suspicious wallet activity flagWithin 1hr

  1. Triggered automatically by anomaly detection or by customer-reported "didn't make this transaction".
  2. If customer-initiated lock (via customer-dispute), wallet is already frozen — verify lock active.
  3. If anomaly-flagged but customer hasn't reported, soft-freeze (limit to existing-counterparty redemptions) and email customer.
  4. Pull last 30d of transactions. Look for: rapid back-to-back redemptions across cities, unusual time-of-day, never-before-seen device.
  5. If clearly fraud → permanently freeze, return any LYMX to original earnings, file a SAR-style internal note.
  6. If false positive → unlock, email customer apology + 50 LYMX inconvenience credit.
Escalate to Kenny + Counsel: any fraud event > $100 in value · any pattern across multiple accounts · any law-enforcement inquiry.

SOP-06: Referral program abuseAudit weekly

  1. Run SELECT * FROM referrals WHERE referee.signup_ip = referrer.signup_ip weekly.
  2. Flag clusters: 5+ referrals from same IP in 24h, or referees who never earn first LYMX.
  3. Manual review per cluster. Some are legit (family, work computer); some are abuse.
  4. For confirmed abuse: clawback the 200 LYMX bonus, email warning, second offense = ban from referral program.

SOP-07: Partner-side gaming (fake activations)Audit monthly

  1. Per Partner Agreement §6, fake activations are grounds for clawback + termination.
  2. Run monthly: any Partner whose recruited Businesses have < 10 transactions in their first 60 days flag for review.
  3. If genuine (Business is just slow), nothing happens.
  4. If fake (no real Business, only the activation), clawback the $500 + send termination notice. Loop in counsel.

Refunds & clawback

When LYMX needs to be reversed — Business return, customer error, fraud event.

SOP-08: Customer requests cash refund of their LYMXCommon confusion

  1. Always: politely decline. LYMX is a promotional credit, not money. Reference disclaimer + refund policy.
  2. Offer alternatives: spend at any LYMX Business, gift to a friend, or convert to a bulk-gift order.
  3. If customer is angry/threatens chargeback: stay polite, document the conversation, escalate to Kenny.
  4. Never offer to refund LYMX as cash, even at our cost. That's the slippery slope to becoming a money transmitter.

SOP-09: Partner sign-up bonus clawback (Business cancels <30d)Auto-triggered

  1. Triggered when a Business signs up + cancels within 30 days. Per Partner Agreement.
  2. Auto-reverse the $500 sign-up bonus from the Direct Partner's pending payouts.
  3. Send Partner the email template signup_clawback_30day explaining what happened.
  4. If Partner had also paid out a downline override, reverse those too.
  5. Log the clawback in commission-backend.

Legal requests

Subpoenas, search warrants, civil discovery, regulator inquiries.

SOP-10: Subpoena or court order receivedWithin 24hr

  1. Do NOT respond directly. Forward to counsel within 24h: brad@bertramcarr.law.
  2. Lock the underlying records (do not delete or modify).
  3. Counsel reviews scope — too-broad orders get a meet-and-confer, not blanket compliance.
  4. If gag order: log internally only. If no gag order: notify the affected user before producing data per our privacy policy.
  5. Disclosure logged in transparency report at next annual update.
Always loop in: Kenny + Counsel within 24h, even for "routine" subpoenas.

SOP-11: NV state regulator inquiryWithin 1 BD

  1. Possible regulators: NV Financial Institutions Division (NRS 671), NV AG, NV Dept of Taxation, FTC.
  2. Forward to counsel and Kenny within 1 business day.
  3. Reference our legal inquiry doc — most questions are anticipated there.
  4. Counsel drafts the response. Kenny reviews. We always respond within the deadline given.

Incident response

Service outage, data exposure, security event. Pre-defined severity levels.

SOP-12: Severity classificationReference

SevDefinitionResponse timePage who
SEV-1Customer data exposed; full service downImmediateKenny + Counsel
SEV-2Wallet QR / POS down for >5 min; partial degradation15 minKenny
SEV-3Single Business affected; minor functionality issue1 hourSam

SOP-13: SEV-1 incident playbookImmediate

  1. 0-15 min: War-room Slack channel opened. Kenny is incident commander. Triage scope.
  2. 15-60 min: Mitigate (rollback, hotfix, or maintenance mode). Update status page.
  3. 1-4 hr: Determine root cause. Email impacted customers if data is involved.
  4. 4-24 hr: Counsel engaged if data involved. Begin breach notification process if applicable.
  5. 24-72 hr: Direct customer + Business notification per our security commitments.
  6. 14 days: Public post-mortem in incident history.
