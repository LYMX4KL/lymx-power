TL;DR — what mattered

Three things, in order: (1) build a 25-prospect list before you pitch anyone, (2) practice the pitch out loud 50 times before your first real call, (3) follow up religiously — most deals close in week 3-4 of the conversation, not week 1. The compound effect of these three is what got me from 0 Businesses in week 1 to Master Partner in 9 months.

Before you start — what I actually had going for me

I was 8 years into a corporate sales role when my dad got sick and I moved back to Las Vegas. I had a B2B SaaS background and zero connection to local Businesses. Most of the things I'd heard about "network marketing" had sounded like garbage.

What flipped it for me about LYMX: the product was real. Local Businesses got real customers. There was no "kit" to buy. No inventory to push. And the model was — once you wrap your head around it — genuinely good for the Business owner. So the question stopped being "can I do this ethically" and started being "can I do this well."

That's the only mental shift you need before week 1. If you can't get there, this isn't the playbook for you.

Week 1Setup before you pitch anyone

Most new Partners ruin their first 30 days by pitching too early. Don't.

  1. Watch the LYMX 101 training videos. All three. Twice. Take notes — what's the math, why does the buy/sell mechanic work, what's the buyer/seller economics. You can't pitch what you can't explain.
  2. Sign your Partner Agreement on day 1. Use the e-signature flow — takes 5 minutes. Don't pitch a single prospect until this is signed. Compliance matters.
  3. Submit your W-9. No W-9, no payout — this trips up new Partners later. Do it now.
  4. Memorize one sentence. The 1-line elevator pitch. Mine: "LYMX is a shared loyalty network for local Businesses — earn at one, spend at any other in the network." Practice it out loud 30 times. You'll need it on demand.
"Week 1 is unsexy. It's also the difference between Partners who hit Bronze in month 1 and Partners who quit in month 3."

Week 2Build the prospect list before any outreach

Goal: 25 names. Not 5. Not 10. Twenty-five Businesses you have at least a weak connection to.

  • Walk-around (3-4 hours) — Walk 8 to 10 blocks of your neighborhood. Note every independent Business that's not a national chain. Coffee, salons, dry cleaners, restaurants, retail, fitness. Aim for 15 from this alone.
  • Phone-contacts pass (1 hour) — Open your contacts. Look for Business owners. Mark anyone you'd be comfortable texting. Most Partners get 5-8 here.
  • Google Maps sweep (1 hour) — Search "coffee shop near [my neighborhood]". Filter for 4+ stars and recent reviews. Note the active ones. Get 4-5 more.

I got to 27 in week 2. Used the LYMX Partner CRM to track each one with notes — owner's name (if I knew it), best time to drop in, who at LYMX they remind me of.

💡

Underrated tactic

For each prospect, write down one specific thing about their Business that's interesting. The pastry case at the bakery. The neighborhood event a salon hosts. You'll use it as your opening line in week 3.

Week 3First calls — but not the ones you think

Week 3 is the inflection point most new Partners blow.

Don't pitch the deal. Don't open with "$850 sign-up". Open with the problem:

"How do you bring back the customer who tried you once and forgot about you?"

If the owner can't give you a great answer (most can't), you're already 80% of the way to a yes. The rest is just walking through the LYMX answer in 5 minutes.

What I actually did in week 3:

  • Sent 10 outreach messages on Tuesday/Wednesday. Mix of email + DM + warm-intro asks. Used the content hub templates as a starting point — but rewrote them in my voice.
  • Walked into 6 Businesses in person. Bought something at each (this matters — you're not a vendor, you're a paying customer). After paying, asked if they had 5 minutes. 4 of 6 said yes.
  • Booked 4 demos for the next week. Used the demo scheduler. Made sure each one was 30 minutes max.
⚠

What I screwed up

Tried to close on the first call with the dry cleaner. Pitched the deal in minute 8 of a 30-minute conversation. He said "interesting, let me think". Lost the deal. Lesson: the first call is to understand their Business. The close is the second call.

Week 4The first close

By week 4 I had 3 actively interested prospects from the previous week's demos. One — the café — said yes on the second call.

What worked:

  • Used the projection calculator live. Pulled up the projection page on my phone, plugged in their estimated monthly volume, walked them through what 6 months would look like. Concrete numbers always beat abstract pitches.
  • Showed the Launch 25 page. The "12 spots open" framing helped — not as scarcity manipulation, but as honest urgency. The first 25 Businesses really do get permanent gold-frame badges and a launch email blast.
  • Walked through the agreement on the iPad. Used sign.html live. Owner signed at the table. Activation kit shipped that night.

Weeks 5-8Scale what's working

The breakthrough in weeks 5-8 wasn't doing something new — it was repeating week 3-4 with bigger numbers.

  • Outreach volume up to 20/week. Half cold, half warm intros via my growing customer base. Customers were now sending me to Businesses — "you should talk to my friend who owns the salon".
  • Always a follow-up booked. If they said "not yet" on call 1, I booked call 2 before leaving. If they said "send me more info", I scheduled the next contact moment, not "I'll get back to you."
  • Started showing up at events. Local small-Business owner meetups. I wasn't there to pitch — I was there to be visible. 3 Businesses came to me unprompted in week 7.

Weeks 9-12Recruit your first downline

By week 9 my own production was starting to plateau on time. I was working 30+ hours/week on LYMX and could maybe do 30-40% more, max. The math said: recruit Partners.

  • Identified 3 candidates from my network — a former colleague who'd been laid off, an insurance broker friend, a real-estate agent. All three had transferable sales skills and time.
  • Pitched the Partner program over coffee. Showed them my 8-week numbers honestly. Let them do their own math.
  • Coached them through their week 1. Same playbook you're reading now — I just walked them through it.

Result by end of week 12: 2 of the 3 signed. One closed her first Business in week 14. The other took until week 18.

💡

The override math is real

3% override on a G1 Partner's production sounds small until you compound it. By month 6, my G1 income equaled what I was making solo in month 3. And it kept growing while I worked the same hours.

5 hard truths I wish someone had told me

  1. Most "no"s are "not yet." The deal closes in week 3-4, not call 1. If you give up on a "not now," you're leaving 60% of your potential income on the table.
  2. You will pitch 50 Businesses to close 12. Roughly 25% close rate. Anything significantly higher and you're talking only to people who'd already say yes.
  3. Your downline is your retirement plan. Production caps at how much time you have. Overrides scale forever. Recruit early.
  4. Your customers are your best Salesforce. A customer who got a 2× LYMX promo at one of your Businesses will tell their friends. Your job is to make Businesses make great customer experiences. The marketing happens on its own.
  5. The compliance stuff isn't optional. Disclose your Partner relationship in every public mention. Use the FTC language from the Academy. The first time you slip and say "passive income" in a public post, you've created a future regulatory problem for everyone.

The 6 tools I used every single day

  • Content hub — pitch script, email templates, objection handling. I rewrote everything in my voice but used the structure.
  • Projection calculator — open it on prospect calls. Math beats marketing.
  • Demo scheduler — replaced "I'll get back to you" with concrete time slots.
  • Downline tree — once I had G1 Partners, this was my coaching dashboard.
