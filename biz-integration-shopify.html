How to connect Shopify

One-time setup. ⏱️ Total: ~14 min

1

Install LYMX from Shopify App Store

From your Shopify admin → Apps → Browse Apps. Search "LYMX". Click Install. Authorize the standard Shopify permissions.

~2 min
2

Connect to your LYMX Business account

If you already have a LYMX Business account, sign in with it. The Shopify store will be linked to it. (If not, you'll create one — same flow as Square setup.)

~2 min
3

Configure online vs physical settings

Decide whether your online store and physical store share customers (yes — most do) or run as separate Businesses. Most operators choose "shared." This is a one-time decision but reversible.

~1 min
4

Set per-product multipliers

In LYMX dashboard → Menu → Shopify products. Walk through your top categories. Set multipliers (default 1×, boost specific items to 2× or 3× to drive attach).

~5 min
5

Enable LYMX at checkout

In Shopify admin → Settings → Checkout. The LYMX module appears in the checkout configuration. Enable. Customize the styling (default already matches your store).

~2 min
6

Test with a $1 order

Place a test order on your store. Confirm LYMX issuance shows in dashboard within 30 sec. Issue a test refund — confirm reversal. Done.

~2 min

Frequently asked questions

Does this work with Shopify Plus?

Yes — and even better. Shopify Plus's Checkout Extensibility lets us deeply integrate LYMX into the checkout flow. Multi-store rollups also supported.

What about Shopify POS (in-store register)?

Shopify POS is supported through the same integration. If your physical store uses Shopify POS, the LYMX flow appears in the Shopify POS app — cashier scans the customer's QR same as any other LYMX integration.

How does this play with Shopify's built-in loyalty (Shopify Rewards)?

They run in parallel without conflict. Most LYMX Businesses turn off Shopify Rewards after 30–60 days because LYMX provides the same use case plus the network. You can keep both if you prefer — they don't conflict at the database level.

What about subscription products (recurring orders)?

Yes. Each recurring billing cycle issues LYMX as a regular transaction. Customer can also redeem LYMX against a future subscription billing if they have balance.

How does it handle international currency?

LYMX is currently US-only. If your Shopify store sells internationally, only USD orders sync to LYMX. International orders fall through without LYMX integration. Multi-currency support is on the roadmap (Q2 2027).

Can I still use Shopify Apps that affect checkout (BNPL, address validators, etc.)?

Yes. LYMX uses Shopify's standard Checkout Extensibility, which is designed to coexist with other apps. We've tested with Klaviyo, Affirm, Bold, Recharge — no conflicts.
