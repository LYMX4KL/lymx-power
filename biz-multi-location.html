The 4 setup decisions

When you're a multi-location operator, there are 4 setup decisions that shape your LYMX experience. Most people get them wrong on the first try. Here's how to think about them.

1One LYMX program, or separate per location?

Your fundamental choice. Determines whether customers earned at location A can redeem at location B without crossing the public network.

One unified program Recommended
All locations share customers, balances, and reporting. Customer earned at Location A redeems at Location B same as at Location A.
✓ Better customer experience · Cross-location attribution visible
✗ Harder to franchise — accounting/ops needs to be unified too
Separate programs per location
Each location is its own LYMX entity. Customer needs separate registrations or relies on the public LYMX network to bridge.
✓ Cleaner if locations have different ownership
✗ Worse customer experience · Cross-location loyalty doesn't work

2Shared menu, or per-location menus?

Determines whether changing a price or item at HQ flows to all locations automatically.

Shared menu If menus are 80%+ similar
Manage menu items once in LYMX. Per-location overrides for prices or stock-out items.
✓ One-place management · Promos scale across locations
Per-location menus
Each location has its own menu. Useful when locations are different concepts.
✓ Best for chains with significant menu variation
✗ Manual work to sync any menu changes across locations

3Shared promotions, or per-location promotions?

Whether a 2× multiplier applies network-wide or only at one location.

Both — set up either Default
Most multi-location operators run global promos (announced network-wide) AND per-location promos (e.g. "happy hour at Location A only").
✓ Maximum flexibility · Test new promos at one location first
Network-wide only
All promos apply to all locations. Simpler to manage but less localized.
✗ Can't test localized strategies

4Single login for all locations, or separate per location?

Whether your manager at Location A also has access to Location B.

Role-based access Recommended
Owner has access to all locations. Each location's manager has access to their location only. Cashiers see only their location's POS.
✓ Privacy + accountability · Manager can't see other locations' settlement
All staff have full access
Everyone with login sees all locations. Simpler to set up.
✗ Too much info for cashiers · Risk of accidental cross-location changes

Common multi-location patterns

Patterns we see operators use successfully. Borrow these as starting points.

🚀

Pilot one location, then scale

Roll LYMX out at one location for 30–60 days. Measure. Apply learnings to the next location. Repeat.

Example: Pilar at Vegas Wellness Group launched at Pilates Henderson first. After 6 weeks of data, rolled out to Summerlin + Spring Valley simultaneously.
🎯

Use multipliers for inter-location traffic

Run a promo at one location to drive customers from another location. Especially powerful when you have a busy + slow location.

Example: "Try our Summerlin location — 3× LYMX on your first visit there." Customers earned at Henderson drive new traffic to Summerlin.
📊

Compare locations weekly

Use the multi-location dashboard to compare per-location metrics weekly. The location that's underperforming usually has a fixable cause (cashier training, signage, hours).

Example: Daniel at Brew & Bean noticed his 4th location had 60% lower LYMX adoption — turned out signage wasn't installed. Fixed in a day.
👥

Cross-train cashiers across locations

If you have 4+ locations, do the cashier training as a single session covering all locations. Cashiers then know they're "one network." Reduces silos.

Example: Vegas Wellness Group does an annual half-day combined-staff training. All 6 locations send cashiers — they learn together.

What NOT to do as a multi-location operator

  • Don't create separate LYMX programs per location. Unless your locations have separate ownership/accounting, this creates a worse customer experience and more bookkeeping work. Use one unified program.
  • Don't run different multipliers without communicating. If Location A is 1× and Location B is 2× without context, customers feel cheated at Location A. Run consistent rates or clearly explain the difference.
  • Don't roll out to all locations on Day 1. Pilot first. Apply lessons. Then scale. The 30-day pilot saves you from making the same mistake at all locations.
  • Don't give all employees access to all locations. Cashiers don't need to see settlement reports. Use role-based access.
  • Don't skip the cross-location reporting. The biggest insight in multi-location is which locations are dragging others down. Look at the comparison reports weekly.
