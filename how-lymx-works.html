How LYMX works

The complete model in plain English — and the hard rules that keep LYMX a loyalty reward, not money.
In one sentence
LYMX is a promotional loyalty credit that is issued and redeemed only by the LYMX platform. It is earned at a participating Business and spent at a participating Business — it is never traded, gifted, purchased, or transferred between people.

The whole flow

1

A Business issues LYMX

Only a participating Business can put LYMX into circulation. It buys LYMX from the platform at 80% of face value, and only at the moment a real customer transaction takes place — at the rate shown on its storefront and in our store. The platform processes the issuance.

2

A customer earns it

The customer's wallet balance increases when the Business issues LYMX on a qualifying purchase. LYMX earned at one Business is spendable at every participating Business in the network.

3

A customer redeems it

The customer spends LYMX as payment at any participating Business (1 LYMX = $0.01 face value). The wallet balance decreases by the amount redeemed. The platform processes the redemption.

4

The Business sells it back

When LYMX is redeemed at a Business, that Business sells those redeemed units back to the platform at the same 80% — and only from a redemption. The platform is the sole counterparty.

Put simply: the LYMX platform is the only issuer and the only redeemer of LYMX. A participating Business is the only party that can acquire LYMX (to issue it) or sell it back (after a redemption), and both happen at 80% of face value, tied to a real transaction. Customers earn and spend — nothing else.

⛔ What is never allowed

  • No trading. LYMX cannot be bought or sold between people, on any exchange, or for cash. There is no secondary market.
  • No gifting. You cannot send, gift, or give your LYMX to another person, wallet, or account.
  • No purchasing by customers. Customers cannot buy LYMX. Only a participating Business acquires LYMX from the platform, at the moment of a transaction.
  • No transfers between accounts. LYMX is tied to the wallet that earned it and is non-transferable (anti-fraud and regulatory — by design).
  • No cash conversion. LYMX is not redeemable for cash, ACH, money order, or any other monetary instrument, from the platform or any Business.
  • No third-party issuance or redemption. Only the LYMX platform issues and redeems LYMX.
Want to share the love with someone? Invite them with your referral link so they earn their own LYMX, or take them to a LYMX Business and pay the bill while they sign up. Those are the supported ways — there is no "send LYMX" feature, by design.

Why these rules exist

Allowing customers to trade, gift, buy, or transfer LYMX would turn it into a money-like instrument, which triggers a different regulatory category (money transmission / stored value) that LYMX is deliberately staying outside of. Keeping LYMX issued and redeemed only by the platform — earned by a customer, spent by that customer, settled between the platform and participating Businesses — keeps it a simple, compliant promotional credit.
