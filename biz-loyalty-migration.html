The migration approach (any platform)

The same 4 phases regardless of which platform you're leaving. Total time: 4–8 weeks.

1

Run both in parallel

30 days. Don't kill the old program — let LYMX prove value first.

2

Inform your customers

Email + signage announcing LYMX. Don't yet say "we're cancelling X."

3

Validate value (week 4)

Compare LYMX traffic vs old program traffic. Decide: keep both? Drop old?

4

Close out the old program

Cancel old program. Migrate any active customer points (optional).

Migrating from Square Loyalty

Square Loyalty is bundled with Square POS. You can disable Loyalty without disabling POS.

Difficulty
Easy
Total time
~30 min + 4 weeks parallel
Customer disruption
None
1

Sign up for LYMX + connect Square integration

Use the Square integration. ~12 min. LYMX layers on top — Square keeps running as your POS.

2

Pause Square Loyalty (don't cancel yet)

In Square Dashboard → Loyalty → Settings → toggle "Active" off. Existing customer points are preserved. Customers can still redeem; new earnings stop.

3

Announce LYMX to your customers

Use the marketing kit. Don't mention Square Loyalty cancellation — just say "we're now on LYMX, here's how it works." Most customers won't notice.

4

Wait 30 days, watch the metrics

By day 30, compare LYMX adoption vs. residual Square Loyalty redemption. For most peers, LYMX captures > 80% of loyalty traffic by week 4.

5

Cancel Square Loyalty + migrate active customer points

In Square Dashboard → Account & Settings → Subscription → cancel Loyalty. Optional: as a goodwill gesture, give existing high-balance Square Loyalty customers a one-time LYMX bonus equivalent to their unused Square points.

⚠️ Gotcha: Square's stamp/punch card data doesn't migrate automatically — but most loyalty data is balance, not historical. The customer email/phone in Square will sync to LYMX automatically through the Square integration, so you don't lose your customer list.

Migrating from Toast Loyalty

Toast Loyalty is a paid add-on ($45/mo). You'll save money + add the network mechanic.

Difficulty
Easy
Total time
~45 min + 4 weeks parallel
Customer disruption
Low (notify them)
1

Sign up for LYMX + connect Toast integration

Use the Toast integration. ~18 min. LYMX is restaurant-grade — handles ticket-line accrual, tip-aware awarding, multi-location.

2

Run Toast Loyalty + LYMX in parallel for 30 days

Both work simultaneously. Customers can use either or both. We've never seen this confuse customers — they redeem whichever is more convenient.

3

Email your customers about the change

Restaurant customers tend to be more engaged with loyalty than retail. Use Toast's customer list export → import into your marketing kit email template. Be transparent about adding LYMX.

4

Cancel Toast Loyalty (after 30 days)

In Toast Web → Settings → Account → Subscriptions → remove Loyalty. Saves $45/mo. Your Toast POS keeps working — only the bundled loyalty add-on is gone.

⚠️ Gotcha: If you have customers with significant Toast Loyalty point balances, give them a heads-up at least 14 days before cancelling, with options to (a) redeem before cancel date, (b) get a one-time LYMX equivalent at your discretion. This is the right move — both ethically and for retention.

Migrating from Yelp Ads (or any pay-per-click)

Yelp Ads aren't really loyalty — they're advertising. But many Businesses replace them with LYMX. Here's how.

Difficulty
Easy
Total time
~10 min
Customer disruption
None
1

Pause Yelp Ads (don't cancel yet)

In Yelp for Business → Ads → Settings → "Pause." Don't fully cancel — pause lets you turn back on if needed. You'll save the monthly Yelp cost.

2

Sign up for LYMX

Use any LYMX integration (Square, Toast, Clover). Your savings from Yelp likely cover the LYMX subscription with money to spare.

3

Compare results after 60 days

Compare your monthly transaction volume + new customer acquisition before vs. after. Most Businesses see equal or better acquisition through LYMX network referrals than through Yelp Ads — at no advertising cost.

4

Decide: keep Yelp or fully cancel?

If LYMX is performing, you can fully cancel Yelp Ads and save 100%. Some Businesses keep a small Yelp Ads spend for general discoverability — that's fine too. Mix and match.

⚠️ Gotcha: Yelp Ads and LYMX solve different problems. Yelp is "discovery for strangers." LYMX is "loyalty network" + "discovery for LYMX customers." Replacing Yelp entirely with LYMX works for most Businesses — but if you depend heavily on stranger walk-ins (e.g., tourists), keep some Yelp budget too.

Migrating from Belly, Fivestars, or other

Standalone third-party loyalty programs. The migration path is similar across all of them.

Difficulty
Medium
Total time
2–3 hours + 4 weeks parallel
Customer disruption
Notify users + offer migration bonus
1

Export your customer list from the old platform

Belly: Settings → Export Customers. Fivestars: Profile → Data Export. Most platforms give you a CSV with name, email, phone, and points balance.

2

Sign up for LYMX

Connect your POS via the appropriate integration. The CSV from your old platform isn't directly imported into LYMX, but you can use it for the customer announcement email.

3

Email old-platform customers about LYMX

Use the CSV. Send a custom email: "We've moved from [Old Platform] to LYMX. To welcome you, we've added [X] LYMX bonus to your wallet. Sign up here." Make the bonus equivalent to their accumulated balance on the old platform.

4

Run both for 30 days, then cancel

Standard 30-day parallel period. Cancel the old platform per their terms (most have 30-day notice). Customers can no longer earn on the old platform but their LYMX wallet has accumulated balance from your migration bonus.

⚠️ Gotcha: Belly / Fivestars stamp cards don't translate to LYMX directly because LYMX uses a different mechanic ($-based, network-wide). Treat the migration as "starting fresh" with a goodwill bonus. Don't try to convert stamp cards 1:1 — convert them to LYMX value (e.g., "5 stamps = $5 LYMX").
