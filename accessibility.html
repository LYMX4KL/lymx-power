Our commitment

LYMX is built so that everyone can earn, spend, and manage their LYMX wallet — including people who use screen readers, keyboard-only navigation, voice control, switch devices, magnifiers, or who have cognitive or motor differences. We aim to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA across getlymx.com, the customer wallet, and the Business POS.

1. Our standard

We measure ourselves against the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1, Level AA — the standard published by the W3C. We don't currently claim a third-party audit certification; we're targeting one in Q4 2026 alongside our SOC 2.

Where the WCAG standard is met or exceeded, we say so. Where we're still working, we say so too. We prefer "we're not there yet" to false claims of compliance.

3. Supported assistive technology

We test LYMX with the following combinations. If you use something else and hit a wall, please let us know.

  • VoiceOver + Safari on macOS (latest 2 major versions)
  • VoiceOver + Safari on iOS (latest 2 major versions) — primary mobile target for the Web Wallet
  • NVDA + Chrome / Firefox on Windows
  • JAWS + Chrome on Windows (commercial)
  • TalkBack + Chrome on Android (latest 2 major versions)
  • Keyboard-only navigation in any modern browser — full feature parity with mouse users
  • Voice Control (macOS / iOS) and Voice Access (Android) for hands-free operation of the wallet

4. Accessibility features built into LYMX

What you'll find without any setup or special mode.

⌨️

Full keyboard navigation

Every page is fully usable with Tab / Shift+Tab / Enter / Escape. Visible focus rings on all interactive elements.

🎨

WCAG-AA color contrast

4.5:1 ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI controls. We check before we ship.

🔍

Browser-zoom-friendly

Layouts reflow gracefully up to 200% zoom without horizontal scrolling.

🏷️

Screen-reader labels

Every form field, button, and image has a descriptive label. Decorative elements are hidden from screen readers.

⏸

No auto-playing motion

Animations respect prefers-reduced-motion. The countdown on the launch page is the only continuously updating element, and it's text.

🌐

Spanish-language version

Full Spanish translation of the homepage at getlymx.com/es. Wallet translation in progress for Q3.

5. Known accessibility issues

What we've found and not yet fixed. Our policy: report it, fix it, log it here.

  • Customer-wallet QR-code refresh — when the QR expires (every 90 seconds), the screen reader doesn't currently announce the change. Fix targeted: May 2026.
  • Partner-tree SVG visualization — the SVG node labels aren't yet exposed to assistive tech. We're adding an alternate "list view" toggle. Fix targeted: May 2026.
  • Two button color combinations — the gold-on-light-yellow CTA on the launch page falls below 3:1 contrast at small sizes. Fix targeted: May 2026.
  • POS keyboard order — the Issue/Redeem tabs don't have an explicit tab order on POS — currently relies on DOM order. Fix targeted: June 2026.

6. Request an accommodation

Some access needs we can't (or don't yet) solve through the product alone. For those, we accommodate one-on-one. Common requests:

  • Large-print printed receipts — Businesses that use our thermal-printer template can switch to 80mm + larger font.
  • Phone-based wallet operations — for people who can't use a smartphone wallet, we can issue an account-linked cardholder ID that works at the counter. Email accessibility@.
  • Alternative formats of policies — large print, audio, or plain-text versions of Terms / Privacy / Refunds available on request.
  • ASL interpreters at events — available with 7 days' notice. Email accessibility@ to request.

7. Report a barrier

The fastest way to make LYMX more accessible is to tell us when something doesn't work. We commit to respond within 2 business days.

  • Email: accessibility@getlymx.com
  • Phone: 702-555-LYMX (5969) — voicemail accepted, including TTY/relay calls
  • Web form: getlymx.com/support

Tell us: where you got stuck, what assistive tech you were using, and what would have helped. The more specific, the faster we fix.

8. Legal & complaint procedure

If you've reported a barrier and feel we haven't responded in good faith, the following formal procedure applies:

  1. Send a written complaint to accessibility@getlymx.com with the subject "Formal accessibility complaint." We respond within 10 business days with a written status update and timeline.
  2. If unresolved, escalate to Kenny Lin (founder & CEO) at kenny@getlymx.com.
  3. You retain the right to file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice (Civil Rights Division) under Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act, the Nevada Commission on Disability Affairs, or in state or federal court. We do not require pre-arbitration of accessibility claims.
