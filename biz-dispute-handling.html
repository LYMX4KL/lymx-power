When a customer disputes a LYMX transaction

Disputes are rare (LYMX network rate is under 0.4%) but they happen. The good news: most are resolved without you owing anything. This is the playbook for handling them quickly and keeping the relationship.

Playbook 1 · Missing LYMX dispute

Customer says they paid but didn't earn. Default stance: assume good faith. Evidence is usually clear from POS receipt.

  1. Pull the POS receipt by date + amount If your POS has search, use customer-stated date + amount. Most POS keep receipts for 90+ days.
  2. Check whether QR was scanned If receipt has "LYMX issued: 0" line item, QR wasn't scanned. Cashier likely missed it. Issue retroactive credit.
  3. If QR was scanned (LYMX issued > 0) Then it's a sync issue between POS and LYMX. Forward to LYMX support — they fix from their end.
  4. Use the dispute response template Don't fight it — refund retroactively. The cost to you is pennies, the cost of losing the customer is real money.
Hi [first name], Thanks for flagging this. I checked our POS — your transaction on [date] for $[amount] is there, but the LYMX scan didn't go through that day (likely staff missed it during a rush). I've issued a retroactive credit of [N] LYMX to your wallet. It should appear within 60 minutes. Sorry for the trouble — appreciate your patience. — [Your name], [Business name]
You don't lose money. Retroactive credits come out of your standard LYMX issuance budget, same as if the cashier had scanned correctly. Net cost to you: ~$0.05 per dollar of LYMX issued.

Playbook 2 · Wrong-amount dispute

Customer claims they earned the wrong number of LYMX. Usually a subtotal vs. total confusion.

  1. Check your LYMX rate setup Dashboard → Settings → LYMX rate. Standard: LYMX issued on subtotal (pre-tax, pre-tip). Confirm yours is set this way.
  2. Compare receipt math Receipt subtotal × your LYMX rate = expected LYMX. If actual LYMX matches that, customer is wrong about expected amount. If actual is less, your POS calculated on something else.
  3. If your math is right, explain it kindly Use the explanation template below. Show your work — most customers accept once they see it.
  4. If your math was wrong, issue the difference Retroactive credit for the gap. No need to refund the original LYMX, just credit the missing portion.
Hi [first name], Looked into this — our LYMX rate at [Business] is [N] LYMX per dollar on the pre-tax subtotal. Your visit on [date]: Subtotal: $[X] · Rate: [N]/$1 · LYMX issued: [Y] This was correct. If you'd expected LYMX on the post-tax total or on tip, that's not how our system is configured (LYMX standard is subtotal). Happy to walk through if you have questions — just reply. — [Your name], [Business name]

Playbook 3 · Unauthorized transaction dispute

Customer says they never visited your Business. Take this seriously — it's either fraud or an attribution error.

  1. Check POS by date + customer wallet ID Find the disputed transaction. What was bought? What time? Who was the cashier?
  2. Pull video if you have it Most POS+camera setups can match a transaction to a video clip. Confirm a customer was there.
  3. If transaction is real and customer just doesn't remember Often they forgot — gift card purchase, kid using their wallet, friend they bought lunch for. Show them the timestamp.
  4. If transaction is suspicious Could be: stolen wallet ID, internal employee fraud, or wrong customer attribution. Flag to LYMX support immediately.
  5. If verified fraud LYMX absorbs the cost — Business doesn't pay if fraud is confirmed. We pursue the fraudster.
Don't accuse the customer. Even if it looks like they're forgetting, lead with curiosity: "I see this transaction on March 12 at 4:14pm — does that ring any bells?" Often they remember. If they don't, refund and move on.
Hi [first name], Thanks for flagging — I checked our records carefully. We do show a transaction at [Business] on [date] at [time] for $[amount]. Our cashier was [name]. Does that timestamp look familiar? If so, we'll let it stand. If not, I'll refund the LYMX immediately and forward to LYMX support to investigate. — [Your name], [Business name]

Dispute do's and don'ts

✅ Do

  • Respond within 24 hours
  • Lead with curiosity, not defense
  • Show the math / receipt clearly
  • Resolve in customer's favor when borderline
  • Issue retroactive credit instead of arguing
  • Forward to LYMX support if you suspect fraud

❌ Don't

  • Accuse the customer of lying
  • Ignore disputes (auto-decided in customer's favor after 7 days)
  • Threaten to ban them from your Business
  • Reveal customer phone/email externally
  • Argue over $1-2 in LYMX (refund and move on)
  • Resolve on POS without LYMX dashboard log (creates audit gap)

FAQ

Who pays for resolved disputes?

If the dispute is resolved in customer's favor and was due to your error: the LYMX credit comes out of your standard issuance budget (~$0.05 per dollar). If it was due to LYMX system error: LYMX absorbs it. If it was fraud: LYMX absorbs and pursues fraudster.

What if I don't respond to a dispute in time?

After 7 days with no response, dispute auto-resolves in customer's favor. The LYMX credit comes out of your account. Don't ignore disputes.

Can I challenge a LYMX dispute decision?

Yes — Settings → Disputes → tap closed dispute → Appeal. Goes to senior LYMX support. Decision is final after appeal review (typically 5 business days).

How does this affect my Business standing?

Dispute rate is monitored. If your dispute rate exceeds 1% of transactions for 3 consecutive months, LYMX support may schedule a review call. Below 1% (which 99% of Businesses are): no flag.

Can I see other Businesses' dispute rates?

No — that's competitive data. You can see network-wide average (currently 0.4%) so you know where you stand.
