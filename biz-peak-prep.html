Don't get caught off-guard on peak days

Friday nights. Mother's Day. Conventions in town. Holiday weekends. The days when you're already busy are also the days LYMX-related issues are most likely to happen — slow QR scans, sync delays, customers grumbling about missing LYMX. This page is your prep playbook.

2 weeks before peak day

Strategy phase
  • Decide if you'll run a peak-day promo2x LYMX, first-50-customers bonus, or no promo. Promo can drive volume — but also adds operational complexity. If short-staffed, skip.
  • Forecast LYMX redemptionBased on prior peaks, expect 18-25% of customers to redeem LYMX. Make sure your settlement reserve covers it.
  • Check staffingOne extra cashier on peak days reduces LYMX scan time bottleneck by 60%. Worth it.
  • Confirm POS hardwareTest scanner, charge tablets, verify printer paper. Order replacements now if needed.
  • Brief your success coachSend a quick "peak day expected on [date]" — they'll have eyes on your dashboard that day.

1 week before

Setup phase
  • Schedule the promo in dashboardDashboard → Promos → New. Set start time, end time, multiplier, daily cap. Live test on 2-3 staff phones to confirm it triggers correctly.
  • Print LYMX signageFree print kit at biz-print-kit. Display "We accept LYMX" + "Earning 2x today" prominently.
  • Train all staff on LYMX flowEven if they know it — refresh. 30-second drill: customer pays, scan QR or take phone number, that's it.
  • Test POS sync end-to-endRun a $1 LYMX test transaction. Confirm in dashboard within 2 minutes. If sync delay > 5 min, escalate to success coach.
  • Stock LYMX merch / freebiesIf your promo includes a freebie (sticker, coupon), order extras now. 30% buffer minimum.
  • Push the promo on socialsUse templates from marketing kit. Post 5 days, 3 days, 1 day before.

Day before peak

Final checks
  • POS health checkRestart POS. Update firmware. Run 2 test transactions. Confirm receipt printer + LYMX line item.
  • Internet backup readyMobile hotspot on standby. If your store wifi goes down mid-rush, you can fail over in under 60 seconds.
  • Print manual fallback receiptsPre-printed slips with phone-number + amount fields. Cashier can hand-write LYMX details if POS goes offline. Apply retroactively from dashboard.
  • Check LYMX dashboard alertsDashboard → Settings → Alerts. Make sure SMS alerts are on so you'll know within 1 minute if sync breaks.
  • Brief opening + closing staffPrint this page, walk them through. Make sure each shift knows the promo and the fallback procedure.
  • Test redemption flowHave a staff member redeem 100 LYMX from their wallet. Confirm UX from customer side.

During peak hours

Live operation
  • Watch dashboard every 30 minutesOne person assigned to glance at LYMX dashboard. Look for sync delays, error spikes, redemption pacing.
  • Use the fast script"Are you on LYMX?" → if yes, scan QR (5 seconds). If no, "Want to start a wallet?" → text them link (2 seconds). Don't pitch — just ask.
  • Phone number fallbackIf QR scan fails twice, ask for phone number. Faster than re-scanning. Customer earns the same.
  • If sync goes downTake the phone number + amount manually. Tell customer "your LYMX will land in 60 minutes." Apply retroactively from dashboard once back online.
  • Don't argue about missing LYMXIf customer says they earned more — issue the credit. Trust them. Costs you pennies, saves the relationship.
  • Log issues for post-mortemNote any LYMX-related incidents on a clipboard. Tomorrow you'll review what to fix.

Real-world peak scenarios + what to do

Three peak situations Vegas LYMX Businesses commonly hit, and the playbook for each.

🌃 Friday-night dinner rush

Solution: 2x LYMX between 5-7pm to push early diners. Cap at 100 customers. Frees later seating.

🎉 Convention week

Solution: enable "first-visit bonus 250 LYMX" for the week. Tourists open wallets, drive new customer growth.

💝 Mother's Day brunch

Solution: pre-paid LYMX gift card promo 2 weeks before. Capture spend, smooth the volume.

After peak day — debrief

Before you forget what went wrong, run a 15-minute debrief the next morning.

  1. Pull peak-day report (Dashboard → Reports → Day report)
  2. Compare LYMX-tagged sales vs total sales — what was your LYMX adoption rate?
  3. Review issues clipboard — what broke, what was fixed?
  4. Update next peak's checklist with anything new you learned
  5. If sync issues: forward incident timestamp to your success coach
