Security practices

What we have in place today, and what's targeted for the next 12 months.

Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) In place

Every connection between your phone, the wallet, the POS, and our servers uses TLS 1.3 — no fallback to older protocols.

Encryption at rest (AES-256) In place

Customer PII, wallet balances, transaction history, and Partner records are encrypted at rest in our managed Postgres database.

Multi-factor authentication on all admin accounts In place

Every staff member with prod access uses hardware-backed 2FA. We don't allow SMS-based 2FA for admin roles (vulnerable to SIM-swap).

Audit logs on every prod access In place

Every database query, deploy, and config change against production is logged with the actor, timestamp, and IP. Logs retained 13 months.

Daily encrypted backups In place

Postgres snapshots every 24h, retained 35 days. Weekly snapshots retained 12 months. All backups encrypted with separate keys from prod.

SOC 2 Type II audit In progress · target Q4 2026

Engaging an audit partner in Q3. Expected report by year-end. Required by larger Business prospects and most institutional partners.

Penetration test (annual) Target Q3 2026

Independent third-party pen test before public launch. We'll publish a summary report (without remediation specifics) here after.

HIPAA / PCI scope avoidance By design

We never collect health information and never touch raw payment cards (Stripe handles all card data). LYMX is intentionally out of PCI/HIPAA scope.

Incident response

How we handle security or availability incidents — the playbook, not the marketing.

If something goes wrong — a vulnerability, a service outage, a data exposure — we follow this playbook:

  1. Within 1 hour: the on-call engineer is paged and starts triage.
  2. Within 4 hours: the incident is classified (Sev1 / Sev2 / Sev3) and an internal Slack war-room is opened.
  3. Within 24 hours: if customer or Business data is affected, our legal counsel is engaged.
  4. Within 72 hours: if data is affected, we notify every impacted customer and Business directly via the email tied to their account.
  5. Within 14 days: we publish a public post-mortem on the status page: timeline, root cause, what we changed.

Frequent questions

From prospect Businesses, customers, and procurement reviewers.

Where is data stored?

Primary data lives in Supabase (managed Postgres) in US-East-1. Daily backups encrypted with a separate key. Cloudflare provides edge caching globally. We don't store customer data outside the United States.

Do you sell customer data?

No. We don't sell, rent, or share customer or Business data with marketers, ad networks, data brokers, or AI training services. Read the privacy policy for the full text.

What about the buyback math — is that exposed publicly?

Aggregate platform stats (total LYMX issued, customers, Businesses) are public — see the coverage map and the changelog. Per-Business or per-customer numbers are private.

Can a Business owner see their customers' data?

A Business sees the customers who transacted with them (transaction list, frequency). They don't see customer activity at other Businesses on the network. That's a hard wall.

Can I request all my data and delete it?

Yes — both. Use the customer-settings page to download or delete your data. We process deletions within 30 days. Backups are scrubbed within 90.

Are you SOC 2 compliant?

Not yet — Type II audit is in progress, targeting Q4 2026. We've already implemented the controls; the audit is the third-party verification step.

Can my procurement team get a security questionnaire response?

Yes. Email security@getlymx.com. We have a CAIQ-Lite response prepared and a SIG-Lite response in draft.

What's your business continuity / disaster recovery target?

RPO (max data loss): 1 hour. RTO (max time to restore): 4 hours. We test quarterly.
