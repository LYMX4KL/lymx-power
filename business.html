How it works for your business

You issue LYMX as a percentage of customer spend. The network does the rest.

1

Set your earn rate

Common: 5 LYMX per $1 spent. You decide. Customers see it on your listing.

2

Customer pays as usual

Your existing POS, your existing menu. LYMX issued automatically through your integration.

3

Customer redeems anywhere

Maybe at you. Maybe at another business. Either way, you only purchase the LYMX units you actually issued — at our 80% wholesale price.

4

We settle monthly

One bill on the 1st: your purchases (units issued) minus our buy-backs (units you accepted) = net amount owed. Transparent. Predictable.

Real economics, worked end-to-end

Let's say you issue 1,000 LYMX ($10 of value) to a customer. Here's what happens.

You as the issuing business

LYMX issued to customer1,000 LYMX
You purchase 1,000 units from LYMX (80% of face)−$8.00
Total cost this transaction−$8.00

In return: a paying customer plus a 1,000-unit promo credit they'll spend somewhere on the network. The 20% "gap" is your built-in customer-acquisition price. No ads bought.

Same 1,000 LYMX redeemed at another business

LYMX accepted from customer at Business B1,000 LYMX
LYMX buys those units back from Business B (80%)+$8.00
Business B net payout+$8.00

Business B gained a customer who didn't know them yesterday. They accepted $10 of LYMX as payment, sold those units back to us for $8 — the $2 gap is the discount they "give" the customer the network sent them. They paid no ads.

The system nets out: half the time you're the issuer paying out, half the time you're the receiver getting paid in. Most businesses settle at roughly net-zero on LYMX flow — meaning your only true cost is the $199/mo subscription, in exchange for a stream of network customers.

Frequently asked

The questions every business owner asks before signing up.

What does LYMX actually cost me each month?
$199/month subscription + a settlement number that's typically near zero. If you issue 14,200 LYMX and customers redeem 8,400 LYMX at your business, the net settlement is roughly $36 (see the worked example above). Your real "marketing cost" only shows up when LYMX is actually redeemed — and that means a real customer transacted.
Is LYMX a cryptocurrency or stored-value account?
No. LYMX is a promotional credit unit used exclusively within the Enterprise ecosystem. It's not cash, not cryptocurrency, and not a stored monetary balance. Redemption value and acceptance are governed by Enterprise participation rules. The disclaimer is on every page of getlymx.com.
What POS systems do you integrate with?
Square, Shopify, Toast, Clover, and Stripe at launch. We also support a receipt-scan fallback for businesses that don't want to integrate at the POS level. Setup happens during the 5-step onboarding.
What if I issue more LYMX than I take in?
That means you've successfully sent customers into the network with money to spend — and other businesses have welcomed them. Your settlement bill on the 1st covers the difference. The 20% baked-in network discount is your customer-acquisition cost for those purchases. Over time most businesses settle close to net-zero; high-traffic businesses (like cafés) typically end up net positive.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. No contracts. Cancel anytime through your dashboard. When you cancel, your outstanding LYMX balance is auto-capped (you can't issue new LYMX during the wind-down) and your getlymx.com listing flips to a "Leaving the LYMX network — still accepting LYMX" banner with days remaining. You continue accepting LYMX for 90 days OR until your balance reaches $0 — whichever comes first. After 90 days, listing is removed from the public site, but you keep accepting LYMX privately until the balance reaches $0. Selling your Business? The new owner can take over the LYMX account as part of the asset transfer if they stay in the network.
How fast can I get listed on getlymx.com?
Most Enterprise businesses go live within 24 hours of completing onboarding. Reviews and detailed listings can be edited at any time from your dashboard.
