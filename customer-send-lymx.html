Why "phone number, not email"? The LYMX wallet works on phone numbers — no email signup required. If you send LYMX to a phone number that doesn't have a wallet yet, it's held for 30 days. We text the recipient with a link to claim by setting up their free wallet (takes 10 seconds).

What happens on the recipient's end

If active wallet
Instant. They get a notification: "[Your name] sent you 2,500 LYMX." Balance updates within 5 seconds. Note (if any) appears in their LYMX activity history.
If no wallet yet
30-day hold. We text them: "[Your name] sent you 2,500 LYMX on the LYMX rewards network. Claim it here: [link]." They tap, set up their free wallet (10 sec), the LYMX is theirs.
If they don't claim in 30 days
LYMX returns to you. Auto-reversal after 30 days. We notify you: "Your gift to [number] wasn't claimed; LYMX returned." Try a different number or follow up directly.
If wrong number
Reverse it within 24 hours. If you typed a typo, go to wallet → Sent → tap transaction → "I sent to wrong person" → confirm. Reverses if recipient hasn't redeemed.

FAQ

Is there a fee for sending LYMX?

Free — both for senders and recipients. No transfer fee. No claim fee. Receiving LYMX is always free.

What's the maximum I can send?

Your full available balance. Pending LYMX (issued in the last 24 hours) can't be sent — only available LYMX.

Can I send LYMX to multiple people at once?

One recipient at a time in the standard flow. For bulk gifts (like office or family), use gift cards which support multi-recipient sends.

Can the recipient send LYMX to someone else?

Yes — once received, LYMX behaves like any LYMX in their wallet. They can redeem at Businesses or send to others.

Will my recipient see my phone number?

They see your first name (and last initial if you set it). Not your phone number. Privacy preserved both directions.

What if I'm sending to someone in a different city / state?

Their wallet works wherever LYMX is. Today that's primarily Las Vegas. As we expand to other cities, their LYMX works in their city's network too.

Can I send LYMX as a recurring monthly transfer (e.g., to a family member)?

Not directly today — but it's on our roadmap. For now, set a phone reminder + manually send. Family wallet (shared balance) is also coming.

Are there any tax implications for sending or receiving LYMX?

For most personal use cases — no. LYMX is loyalty rewards, not a financial transfer. If you're sending large amounts as effective gifts, consult your tax advisor on annual gift-tax exclusion limits.
