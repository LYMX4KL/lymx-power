No "send money" form here — and that's intentional
LYMX is loyalty rewards, not cash. There's no input field to type a phone number and an amount because customer-to-customer LYMX transfers aren't allowed (regulatory + by design). But there ARE four real ways to share value with someone — scroll down to "Want to treat someone?" for actionable options.
🔒

LYMX rewards stay in your wallet

LYMX is a personal rewards balance. You earned it at a participating Business — it stays linked to you. It's not transferable to other customers, and it isn't cash.

Why LYMX isn't transferable between customers

LYMX is loyalty rewards, not currency. Allowing customer-to-customer transfers would turn LYMX into a money-like instrument, which triggers a different regulatory category we're deliberately staying outside of (Nevada NRS 671). Keeping LYMX inside your wallet — earned by you, spent by you — keeps the platform simple, fast, and compliant.

Simple rule: You earn LYMX at Business A, you spend LYMX at any LYMX Business in the network. That's the whole flow.

Want to treat someone? Here are real options.

🎁Gift cards (coming soon)

Buy a LYMX gift card from the platform with your credit card. Send to anyone. They claim it as their own LYMX balance. Different from transferring — the gift card is a fresh purchase from us.

📍Visit a LYMX Business together

Take a friend out. You pay the bill (cash or card). Both of you earn LYMX at the visit (they sign up at the counter — takes 30 seconds).

🤝Invite them to LYMX

Share your referral link. When they sign up and make their first purchase, you both earn a 100 LYMX bonus. Share your link →

💸Pay them back in cash

If you owe someone money, send them cash or use Venmo/Zelle. LYMX is your loyalty balance — not a money-transfer service.

📌 Non-transferable, by design.
LYMX rewards are issued to a specific customer wallet and are not transferable to another customer's wallet. The only exception is the formal gift-card flow (purchased fresh from the platform). Attempting unauthorized transfers will be detected and the affected balances may be reversed.

This rule is part of why LYMX is classified as a promotional credit (not stored value, not cryptocurrency, not money transmission). See Terms of Service and Privacy for the full text.
