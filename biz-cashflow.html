How LYMX affects your cash flow

Understanding the timing — when LYMX hits your books, when it doesn't, and how to plan for the wholesale-cost impact. Most Businesses overestimate the cash-flow impact; the reality is usually small and predictable.

The 60-second summary

  • Settlement is weekly — every Wednesday for the prior week's activity
  • You receive net — LYMX issuance cost (~3% network fee) auto-deducted; redemption cost (80% when LYMX issued elsewhere is redeemed at your Business) auto-deducted
  • The 80% rule is the biggest line — but only kicks in when other Businesses' LYMX is redeemed at yours; usually small in early months
  • Cash flow is positive — you keep 100% of customer purchases (cash/card) immediately; LYMX cost is settled separately, weekly

1. Settlement statement — the line items

Every Wednesday, your dashboard shows a fresh settlement statement for the prior week. It breaks down into 4 line items.

Line 1 — LYMX issued at your Business

Total LYMX you awarded customers this week. Each LYMX has a $0.01 redemption value. You pay the network ~3% of issuance value as a service fee.

Line 2 — LYMX redeemed at your Business

Customers spent LYMX with you this week. Two sub-cases:

  • Customer redeeming LYMX they earned at YOUR Business: No cost to you — they're spending against credit you'd already paid the network for at issuance.
  • Customer redeeming LYMX earned ELSEWHERE: You pay 80% of redemption value to the network at settlement (the 80% Redemption Cost Rule). The remaining 20% is your customer-acquisition cost — you get the customer, you eat 20% of their LYMX value.

Line 3 — Subscription

$199/mo flat (or $249 / $299 / etc. for multi-location). Charged once a month, deducted from settlement.

Line 4 — Network fees

3% LYMX issuance fee + any platform service fees. Auto-applied.

In this example, LYMX costs the coffee shop ~1.6% of weekly revenue. The customer-acquisition lift (12-18% incremental revenue typical) more than offsets that.

3. The cash flow impact in plain English

What's going OUT every week

  • Weekly settlement deduction — the net of network fees + redemption cost + subscription portion. For a $10K/week business: ~$150-250 typical.
  • That's it. No upfront LYMX inventory to buy. No pre-funding. No deposits.

What's coming IN every day

  • 100% of cash/card customer purchases — these never touch LYMX. Whatever your customer pays at the register goes straight to your POS settlement (Square/Toast/etc.).
  • LYMX is deducted SEPARATELY at the weekly settlement — not from each transaction.

The result

Your daily cash flow looks the same as it did before LYMX. The LYMX impact appears as a single weekly line item in your books — predictable, easy to forecast, easy to budget.

Common misconception: "LYMX takes a cut of every sale." It doesn't. LYMX is a separate weekly settlement against your account, not a per-transaction deduction. Your POS keeps doing what it does.

4. How to plan for it in your books

Treat LYMX as a marketing line item

Most LYMX Businesses categorize the weekly settlement deduction as "marketing & promotions" — comparable to how you'd categorize Yelp ads or Google ads. It's a customer-acquisition spend.

Budget rule of thumb

  • 2-3% of LYMX-tagged revenue — typical total LYMX cost in early months
  • 1-2% of total revenue — when LYMX represents 50% of customers (mature state)
  • Plus the $199/mo flat — fixed regardless of activity

For QuickBooks / Xero

Auto-sync handles this. See QuickBooks integration or Xero integration. They categorize LYMX as a marketing expense by default.

Tax treatment

LYMX cost is fully deductible as marketing & promotions expense. See biz-tax-guide for full breakdown.

Common scenarios

"I'm new to LYMX — what's the cost like in week 1?"

Tiny. You haven't built up cross-redemption volume yet, so 80% costs are near zero. Most week-1 settlements are: subscription portion + 3% network fee = under $80 for a small Business.

Expect $50-100 first-week settlement deduction, then growing as your customers earn + spend across the network.

"What if I'm cash-strapped and can't afford the weekly deduction?"

Weekly settlement reflects net of revenue earned vs. cost owed. You'll never owe more than you've earned in LYMX activity that week — except for the flat subscription portion.

If you're worried, talk to your success coach. We can defer subscription payment up to 30 days during cash-flow crunches. Used judiciously.

"My LYMX cost spiked this week — what happened?"

Most spikes are caused by: (1) a viral promo bringing in heavy LYMX redemption from other Businesses' customers, (2) a refund batch hitting LYMX too, (3) a one-time multi-location add fee.

Run reconciliation report (Dashboard → Reports → Settlement detail). It shows line-by-line where the cost came from.

"Can I delay LYMX issuance to manage cash flow?"

You can pause LYMX issuance temporarily (Dashboard → Settings → Pause issuance for X days). Customers will see your listing as "Currently not issuing LYMX — still accepting redemption."

Use sparingly — pausing hurts customer trust. Better to address cash flow root cause.

"Do I get paid faster with same-day settlement?"

LYMX settlement is weekly, not per-transaction. Your daily POS sales (Square/Toast/etc.) are NOT affected — those settle on your POS's normal schedule (next-day for Square, etc.).

There's no LYMX cash-flow speed-up to opt into. The weekly settlement is the schedule.

FAQ

What if I owe MORE than I earned in LYMX activity that week?

Only happens if you ran a heavy promo with very low issuance but high redemption. Typically the deficit is under $50. Auto-deducted from your settlement account; if account is empty, deferred to next week's settlement.

Does LYMX charge interest on deferred settlements?

No interest charged within standard 30-day cash-flow grace. Past 30 days: 1.5%/month service fee. We'll always notify you well before that triggers.

Can I prepay several months of subscription?

Yes — prepay 12 months for a 5% discount ($199 × 12 = $2,388 → $2,269). Email biz-support to set up annual prepay.

What happens to settlement if I pause my account?

Your account can be paused for up to 90 days (vacation, renovation, etc.). During pause: no subscription charge, no LYMX issuance, but customers can still redeem if you opt in. Settlement runs as normal for any redemption activity.

How is multi-location settlement handled?

Single weekly settlement statement covers all locations. You can see per-location breakdown in dashboard. One ACH payout to your designated account.
