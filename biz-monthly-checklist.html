30 minutes a month — that's all this takes

Most LYMX Businesses set autopilot and run their loyalty rewards in the background. But a 30-minute monthly check keeps things tuned. Here's the standing rhythm that takes the worry out of LYMX.

The promise

If you do this 30-minute checklist once a month, you'll catch settlement issues before they grow, find promo opportunities you'd miss, retain customers other Businesses lose, and stay ahead of any disputes. Owners who skip it spend 3-5x more time firefighting later.

1. Numbers review

5 minutes
  • Open Business dashboard, last 30 days viewQuick glance at: total LYMX-tagged sales, customer count, average ticket size.
  • Compare vs. prior 30-day periodUp or down? More than 15% swing in either direction is worth investigating.
  • Check LYMX adoption rateOf all your customers this month, what % were on LYMX? Healthy: 35%+. Below 25%: cashiers may be forgetting to ask.
  • Spot any odd daysOne Tuesday with 0 LYMX activity? Could be sync issue you didn't catch. Flag for biz-troubleshoot.
  • Top 5 LYMX customers listNote who they are. These are your VIPs — when they lapse, you'll want to know fast.

2. Settlement audit

7 minutes
  • Pull last month's settlement statementDashboard → Reports → Settlement detail.
  • Verify subscription line: $199/mo (or $249/$299/etc. for multi-location)If different, contact biz-support. Mistakes are rare but happen during multi-location adds.
  • Check 80% redemption-cost lineIf LYMX issued elsewhere was redeemed at your Business, you pay 80% at settlement. Verify the math: redeemed amount × 0.80.
  • Confirm 3% network fee charged correctly3% on LYMX issuance, deducted at settlement. Should match your issued LYMX × 0.03.
  • Reconcile against POS reportsPOS LYMX-tagged sales should match dashboard within ~2-3% (refunds, tip-vs-subtotal differences).
  • Flag anything unexpectedUse settlement-dispute within 30 days of receiving statement.

3. Promo planning

8 minutes
  • Review last month's promosDid the 2x Tuesday lift Tuesday revenue? Did the first-50 customer bonus actually fill seats?
  • Schedule next month's promoUse biz-promo-planner calendar template. Aim for 1 promo/month minimum.
  • Always set a daily cap"First 50 customers" or "$X LYMX max." Open-ended promos are how Businesses lose 4-figures in a weekend.
  • Time it for slow days/hoursPromote into slow time, capture peak time at full margin.
  • Coordinate with seasonal calendarMother's Day in May? Halloween costumes in October? See Vegas peak calendar for which dates lift naturally.
  • Push promo on socials with templatesUse biz-marketing-kit 5 days, 3 days, 1 day before.

4. Customer + staff check

10 minutes
  • Lapsed customer listDashboard → Customers → Lapsed (auto-segmented). Anyone who used to come weekly hasn't visited in 6+ weeks?
  • Send "we miss you" through LYMXMarketing → "Send to my LYMX customers" → Lapsed segment. Attaches a small bonus (100 LYMX) to entice return. Goes through LYMX wallet, not your direct email.
  • Top 5 VIPs — personal touchIf any of them haven't visited in a while, find a way to reach them. A phone call, text, handwritten note.
  • Cashier check-inSpot-check: are cashiers still asking "are you on LYMX?" before payment? Run a 1-day mystery audit if unsure.
  • Staff feedback30 seconds with each cashier: any LYMX-related friction this month? They see it before you do.
  • Update staff training if neededIf sync issues / scanner problems came up, quick refresher. Use biz-troubleshoot as reference.

🚩 Red flags this rhythm catches early

  • Settlement amounts off by more than 5% (sync bug or misconfiguration)
  • LYMX adoption rate dropping (cashiers losing the habit of asking)
  • VIPs going dark for 6+ weeks (relationship maintenance opportunity)
  • Sync issues on specific days (unnoticed POS-LYMX gap)
  • Promos that didn't lift the metric (stop running ineffective ones)
  • Cashier confusion on edge cases (refunds, splits, gift cards)

Always-on tasks

  • Daily: Glance at dashboard during your usual close-out. 30 seconds.
  • Daily: If a customer mentions a LYMX issue, log it (use biz-troubleshoot) — don't let it go unfixed.
  • Weekly: Verify weekly settlement hits your bank. Bank holidays push by 1 day.
  • Weekly: Skim customer reviews on Yelp/Google — any LYMX-specific complaints?
  • Quarterly: Review with your success coach. They have data you don't.

FAQ

What if I miss a month?

One missed month is fine — most issues build slowly. Two consecutive misses, and you're in firefight mode. Get back on rhythm or schedule a coach call.

Can my manager run this checklist instead?

Most steps yes — Manager role can see all the data. But settlement audit + cancellation/billing changes are Owner-only. See biz-staff-roles.

How does this differ from quarterly business reviews (QBRs)?

This is operational. QBRs (with your success coach) are strategic — comparing you against benchmarks, planning growth. Both matter; they're not the same thing.

Should I run the checklist for each location separately?

Yes for multi-location. Each location has its own LYMX adoption + settlement detail. The dashboard supports per-location filter.
