From Bronze to Master

The setup

Sienna Vega had spent 12 years in B2B SaaS sales — Salesforce, then a mid-market account exec at HubSpot, then a frustrating year at a fintech startup that pivoted twice. By summer 2025, she was burned out on quotas, layoffs, and pretending to care about pipeline calls.

She'd known Kenny for years through Vegas restaurant industry friends. When Kenny pitched her on LYMX — the early version, just a kitchen-table idea — she said no. "Sounded like another loyalty program. I'd seen 50 of them."

Then, six months later, Kenny called her again. The model had crystallized. Brew & Bean had signed. He needed someone who could pitch.

"I told him 'No, I'm not selling another SaaS thing.' He said 'It's not SaaS. It's a network. Once it gets started, it builds itself, and you build with it. Just come look at the dashboard for one Business that's been on for 2 weeks.' I looked. The cross-redemption was already happening. I was in."

Week 1 — humbling

Sienna was Bronze tier from October 1, 2025. She thought her SaaS sales chops would translate immediately. She tried to pitch LYMX like she'd pitched HubSpot — features, ROI calculator, decision-maker mapping.

It didn't work.

She spent week 1 walking into Henderson Businesses, pitching for 8 minutes about LYMX features, and watching owners' eyes glaze over. Zero Businesses signed.

"Small Business owners don't care about feature lists. They care about whether you understand their actual day. Whether you've seen what they've been through. Whether your idea respects their time."

The pivot

By week 2, Sienna had thrown out her SaaS playbook and started over. She rewrote her opening. Instead of "I'm here to talk about LYMX," she started with: "I'm here to learn about your Business. I'll tell you about LYMX in the last 5 minutes if you think it might be a fit."

That single change unlocked everything. Owners opened up. They told her about their lapsed customers, their failed punch-card programs, their Yelp ad budgets that didn't work. By the time Sienna got to "here's how LYMX would help" — they were already nodding.

First sign-up: October 18, 2025. Brew & Bean's neighbor, Sage & Stone Bakery. Cristina Vega (no relation, friend of friend) at Salon Verde, the case study above. Then a flurry — Sienna signed her first 5 Businesses by November 22.

Earning Founding 25 status

On November 22, 2025, Sienna became the FIRST Founding 25 Partner. Lifetime perks locked: $750 sign-up bonus per future activation (vs $500), 11% Direct override (vs 9%), permanent fee waiver, direct line to Kenny.

"Founding 25 felt like the moment I'd actually committed. Up until then, I was 'trying it.' Hitting 5 activations made it real. The locked perks meant I was building something that compounds for me — not just a job."

The Master Playbook (born from this journey)

Around month 4, Sienna realized she was answering the same questions from new Partners over and over. So she wrote it down. The 90-day Master Playbook — now official LYMX training material — is her exact system, codified.

Highlights from her playbook:

  • Days 1-30: Discovery only. No pitching. Build a target list of 50 Businesses. Visit 8-10/week. Listen way more than you talk.
  • Days 31-60: First closes. Start with the lowest-friction Businesses (single-owner, your-POS-supported, growth-stage). Aim for 3-5 sign-ups in this window.
  • Days 61-90: Building your downline. Recruit 1-2 G1 Partners. Start to think about your tree, not just your Direct activations.

Sienna's most important insight, in her own words

"The mistake every former-SaaS Partner makes (including me): treating each Business sign-up as a transaction. It's not. It's a relationship that compounds for years. I'll earn from Brew & Bean's monthly subscription for 5+ years. Treat Year 1 sign-ups like founding partnerships, not closed deals. The slow effort pays you forever."

The financial picture

Sienna's $97k YTD breaks down approximately:

  • $28,500 — sign-up bonuses ($750 × 38 Direct activations)
  • $48,200 — Direct subscription overrides (11% of $199 × 38 Businesses × varying months)
  • $15,900 — G1/G2/G3 generation overrides (across her 14 Partners + their downline)
  • $4,400 — LYMX-side commissions (network-redeemable, not cash)

Her ongoing run-rate today is approximately $11,800/month in cash commissions, growing as her tree expands. She works ~20-25 hours/week now — much less than the 60+/week she did at HubSpot.

What Sienna would tell new Partners

If you walk into every meeting with that mindset, you'll outperform every Partner with a SaaS sales background. Including me when I started."
