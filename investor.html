The problem

Loyalty programs are quietly broken — and small Businesses pay the price twice.

Customers stockpile points they never use. Small Businesses pay for ad impressions while chains sit on captive rewards balances. Both sides lose.

I earned hundreds of thousands of miles with a major airline. The cash price was always cheaper than the points price. I never redeemed one.
300,000 points · 0 redeemed · $0 real value
I had tens of thousands of points at a fast-food chain. The catalog only had items I didn't want. I never spent a single one.
~80,000 points · 0 redeemed · $0 real value
Hotel rewards keep raising the points needed for the same room. The "value" you "earned" gets quietly devalued every year.
25k → 60k points for 1 night · −58% spending power

For independents, the other side of the same coin: $2,000+/month on Yelp + Google + Facebook just to be visible — most of it spent on impressions, not paying customers. Chains get the network leverage; independents get the bills.

The solution

One credit. Fixed value. Spendable anywhere on the network.

LYMX is a shared promotional credit unit. Worth a fixed $0.01 forever — devaluation is structurally impossible. Earned at any participating Business. Redeemable at any other. The network IS the rewards program.

🛍️

Customer earns

Customer spends $20 at a coffee shop. Shop issues 100 LYMX (5 LYMX/$1 locked rate). Shop purchases the units from LYMX at 80% wholesale ($0.80).

🚪

Customer redeems

Customer goes to a different Business — say a salon — and pays for $1 of service with 100 LYMX. Salon accepts at face value.

⚖️

LYMX settles

Salon sells those units back to LYMX at the same 80% wholesale ($0.80). Salon absorbs the 20% gap as the discount. Coffee shop got a paying customer; salon got a customer the network sent them.

Naturally recurring

$199/month subscription is the foundation. Once a Business is on the network, churn is structurally low — leaving means losing access to the network's customer pool.

🌐

Network effects

Each new Business makes existing Businesses more valuable (more places customers can spend). Each new customer makes Businesses more valuable. Both sides reinforce.

⚖️

No money handling

Buy/sell mechanic — LYMX is the issuer, seller, and buyer-back of its own promotional credit unit. Designed to stay outside money-transmitter classification (NV NRS Chapter 671).

The flywheel

Each Business brings ~1,500 new customers into LYMX every month.

Customer acquisition isn't paid — it's structural. Every customer that walks into a LYMX Business gets LYMX issued at checkout, which signs them up to the network. The Business is the customer-acquisition channel.

🏪

Per typical Business / month

$100K volume ÷ $20 avg ticket = 5,000 customers/month. 70% repeat → 1,500 brand-new customers per Business per month, each issued LYMX at checkout.

🌐

10 Businesses joining

~15,000 new LYMX customers entering the network in their first month — all already engaged with at least one Business and holding redeemable LYMX. Compounds month-over-month.

💸

LYMX's customer-acquisition cost

$0. Every customer arrives via a Business that paid us $850 to sign up. Customer acquisition is structurally bundled with Business acquisition.
